## Perspective Rotation (Top Idea: Community Story Kiosks)
- End User (Rural Bengali speaker): Needs simple interface, works without internet, culturally relevant
- Adversary (Commercial radio): Will argue this competes unfairly, may lobby against
- Regulator (MIB): Requires registration as community resource, not broadcast
- Financier (Grant org): Wants measurable community impact, sustainability plan
- Operator: Needs durable hardware, simple maintenance, local support

## Second-Order Effects
- Short-term: Local voices gain platform, cultural preservation begins
- Long-term: Bengali language vitality increases, oral history documented
- Then: Community storytelling culture revitalized
- And then: Model replicable for other languages/regions
