## How to Set Up Alerts

### Option 1: Email Newsletters (Easiest)
1. Visit each government website
2. Look for "Subscribe", "Newsletter", or "Notifications"
3. Enter your email
4. Confirm subscription
5. Check inbox weekly for updates

### Option 2: RSS Feeds (If available)
1. Look for RSS feed icon on government websites
2. Use an RSS reader (Feedly, Inoreader, The Old Reader)
3. Add feed to your reader
4. Notifications come to your reader automatically

### Option 3: Manual Monitoring
1. Set a calendar reminder (every Monday)
2. Visit each government website
3. Check "News" or "Announcements" section
4. Note any changes in spreadsheet

### Option 4: Social Media
Some government agencies announce changes on:
- Twitter/X
- LinkedIn
- Government social media accounts
- Follow and enable notifications

## Priority Alerts

**If you see changes in these areas, publish immediately:**
- Asylum procedure changes (affects vulnerable populations)
- Family reunification rules (time-sensitive)
- Visa category eliminations (people need to know)
- Major fee increases
- Processing time increases (>6 months)
- Court rulings affecting eligibility

**Can wait for regular schedule:**
- Minor procedural clarifications
- Fee decreases
- Processing time decreases
- Administrative updates (office addresses, hours)
