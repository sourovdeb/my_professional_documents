## Post Content

**Good news for families:** Both the European Union and France have streamlined family reunification procedures for asylum seekers and refugees. If you're waiting to bring family members to join you in Europe, here's what's changed and what you need to do.

### What Changed

The EU's revised family reunification rules (implemented across member states) now:
- Speed up processing for spouses and dependent children
- Expand documentation flexibility for people fleeing conflict
- Clarify appeals procedures when applications are denied
- Reduce delays for people with subsidiary protection status

France's OFPRA has also updated its family reunification guidance to align with these EU standards, cutting typical processing times from 9–12 months to 6–9 months in most cases.

### What This Means for You

**If you're a refugee or asylum seeker in France or the EU:**

- **You can apply** to bring your spouse and dependent children (under 21) without delay
- **Income requirements** are now lower and more flexible if you're on government assistance
- **Housing requirements** are clearer—you don't need to own a home (rent is fine)
- **Processing time:** Expect a decision within 6–9 months (check your country for specifics)
- **If denied**, you have 30 days to appeal; the appeals process is now simpler

### What to Do Right Now

- [ ] Gather documents: birth certificates, marriage certificate, police clearance for family members
- [ ] Confirm your residence status (refugee, subsidiary protection, or pending asylum)
- [ ] Check your country's official website for specific income/housing rules
- [ ] Contact OFPRA (France) or your national immigration office
- [ ] Apply immediately—processing takes time, so don't delay
- [ ] Keep copies of everything you submit
- [ ] Save your case number and contact info

### Important Limits

- You **cannot** sponsor extended family (parents, siblings, cousins)—only spouse and dependent children
- Rules vary slightly by country, so verify with official sources
- Some countries have caps on family reunification numbers
