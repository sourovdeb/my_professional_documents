# Microblog Post Templates

Use these templates to ensure consistency and speed up publishing when migration laws change.

---

## Template 1: "What This Means for Applicants"

**When to use:** Major policy changes, new eligibility criteria, procedure updates

**Structure:**

```markdown
# [Policy Area]: [Brief Description of Change]

**Update Date:** [Date of change/announcement]

---

## What Changed
[2-3 sentences: What specifically changed in the law/policy]

## What This Means for Applicants

If you're applying for [visa type / status]:

- **Timeline:** [How long it now takes]
- **Eligibility:** [Who qualifies; what changed]
- **Cost:** [Application fees, if changed]
- **Documents needed:** [Key new/modified requirements]
- **Processing location:** [Where to apply; if changed]

### Affected Groups
- Asylum seekers
- Family reunification applicants
- [Other relevant groups]

## Important Notice
**This is informational only, not legal advice.** Consult a qualified immigration lawyer before applying.

## Next Steps
- [ ] Check official government website for your country
- [ ] Confirm you meet new eligibility criteria
- [ ] Gather required documents
- [ ] Schedule appointment with immigration office
- [ ] Seek legal advice if eligibility is unclear

## Resources
- [Government official page](link)
- [Local legal aid](link)
```

- [ ] Consult a lawyer if your case may be affected

## Publication Checklist

Before publishing ANY post:

- [ ] **Accuracy:** All dates, timelines, and procedures verified against official sources
- [ ] **Legality:** Legal review completed; lawyer approved
- [ ] **Accessibility:** Written in plain language (avoid jargon)
- [ ] **Attribution:** All sources cited with official links
- [ ] **Disclaimer:** "This is informational only, not legal advice" clearly visible
- [ ] **Contact info:** Legal aid / NGO contact info included
- [ ] **Links work:** All URLs tested and valid
- [ ] **Current:** Information is not outdated (check publication date)
- [ ] **Formatting:** Consistent with other posts (headers, lists, bold text)

---

## Tone & Style Guide

- **Use "you" and "your"** — speak directly to readers
- **Plain language** — avoid legal jargon; define necessary terms
- **Active voice** — "You must submit" not "Applications must be submitted"
- **Action-oriented** — give readers concrete next steps
- **Empathetic** — acknowledge that immigration is complex and stressful
- **Neutral** — no opinion or editorializing; facts only
- **Scannable** — use headers, lists, and bold to help readers find what they need
