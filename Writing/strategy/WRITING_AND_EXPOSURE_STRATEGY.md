## The 500-Word Daily Practice

Write 500 words every day. Do not publish every day. The rule:

- **Write daily** — even badly, even unfinished
- **Publish 3x per week** — Monday, Wednesday, Friday works well
- **Let the best days become the posts**

500 words takes 20–40 minutes. Set a timer. Start with a sentence you actually think, not a sentence you think sounds good.

### Format for a quick 500-word post:
1. One thing that happened or one idea you had (2–3 sentences)
2. What it made you think (150 words)
3. What you actually know about it from experience (200 words)
4. What it means for the reader (100 words)
5. One question to end on (1 sentence)

That's it. You can do this every morning before your daughter wakes up or after medication settles.

>
> I have a piece — [title] — about [one sentence description]. It's [word count] words and hasn't been published elsewhere.
>
> Would you be interested in seeing it?
>
> Sourov Deb
> [Substack link] | [LinkedIn]

Keep it under 150 words. Attach nothing. Wait 4–6 weeks before following up.
