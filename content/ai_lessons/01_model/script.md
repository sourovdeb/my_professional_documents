# AI Lessons, Episode 1: What Is a "Model"?

*A 2–5 minute lesson · Series: Core AI Terms in Plain English*
*Format: video script / read-aloud lesson · Created by Sourov Deb*

---

## Cold open (10 seconds)

> "You already do this every day and don't call it AI. Let's fix that."

**[DOODLE 1 on screen: a hand hovering over a dropdown menu]**

---

## 1. The everyday version (30 seconds)

Think about your web browser. In Settings, you can pick your **default search engine** — Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo. Same question, same browser, but each engine gives you a slightly different set of answers.

An AI **model** is that same idea, one layer down. It's the "engine" doing the thinking behind an AI tool. Swap the model, and the same question can come back faster, slower, cheaper, or smarter.

**[DOODLE 1: browser dropdown "Search engine: Google ▾" next to a Mistral Studio dropdown "Model: Mistral Large ▾", with an arrow between them labeled "Same idea!"]**

---

## 2. The plain-English definition (20 seconds)

> A **model** is the trained "brain" an AI app runs on. One app can offer several models — small and fast, or large and smart — the way one browser can offer several search engines.

No jargon needed beyond that. Everything else in this series (skill, agent, memory, hook) is something you *add on top of* a model — so this is where the series starts.

---

## 3. The kitchen analogy (30 seconds)

Picture a recipe card — that's your question, or "prompt." Now hand that same recipe to two different chefs:

- **The intern (small model):** fast, cheap, gets simple orders right — perfect for a quick sandwich.
- **The head chef (large model):** slower and pricier, but handles the complicated, multi-step dish.

Same recipe, same kitchen, different chef — different result. That's exactly what happens when you switch models.

**[DOODLE 2: one recipe card with two arrows pointing to two chef stick-figures — a small one labeled "fast/simple" making a sandwich, a tall one labeled "smart/slow" plating a fancy dish]**

---

## 4. See it live in Mistral Studio (60–90 seconds)

1. Open **Mistral Studio** and start a new chat or playground session.
2. Find the **model picker** — usually a dropdown near the top of the screen (e.g., *Mistral Small*, *Mistral Large*, *Codestral*).
3. Ask it something simple, like "Summarize this paragraph in one sentence," using **Mistral Small**. Notice the answer comes back almost instantly.
4. Switch the dropdown to **Mistral Large** and ask the *same* question. The answer may take a beat longer, but it's often more nuanced.
5. That dropdown is the whole lesson in one click: **you're not changing the app, you're changing the "chef" doing the thinking.**

**[DOODLE 3: three brain-in-a-box characters, small/medium/large, in a row under a big dropdown arrow — a hand reaching to pick the medium one]**

---

## 5. Why this matters at work (20 seconds)

Just like you wouldn't use a sledgehammer to hang a picture frame, you don't need the biggest, most expensive model for a quick task. Match the model size to the job: small models for quick drafts and simple lookups, large models for complex analysis or writing that needs real nuance.

---

## 6. Recap (10 seconds)

> A **model** = the engine/chef behind the AI. Same app, different model, different speed and skill — pick the one that fits the job, exactly like picking a search engine in your browser.

**[DOODLE 1 returns, now with a checkmark]**

---

## User notes (for whoever's presenting or reading this)

- Total runtime if read at a natural pace: **~3 minutes**. The Mistral Studio walkthrough (§4) is the part to slow down for — it's the "aha" moment.
- If presenting live, actually open Mistral Studio and click the model dropdown on screen instead of describing it — seeing the label change is more convincing than hearing about it.
- Swap "Mistral Studio" for whatever AI tool your audience already has open, if needed — the model-picker pattern (a dropdown near the top of the screen) is nearly universal across AI apps.
- Common mix-up to head off: a **model** is not the same as an **app**. Mistral Studio is the app (the kitchen); Mistral Large/Small are the models (the chefs) you can pick inside it.
