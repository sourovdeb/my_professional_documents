I did the CELTA — Cambridge's gold standard teaching certification.

And I think it has a serious problem that nobody's talking about.

Quick context: CELTA is 120 hours of intensive training to become a certified English language teacher. It's respected worldwide. The tutors are great. The syllabus is solid.

But two of its three assessment pillars are now almost trivially easy to cheat.

Here's what I mean:

→ VIDEO OBSERVATIONS
You watch pre-recorded lessons and answer detailed questions. In theory, great. In practice? Run the video through an AI transcription tool, feed it the questions, get perfect answers in 5 minutes. Done.

→ WRITTEN ASSIGNMENTS
4 essays of ~1,000 words each. Collect some basic info about your students in the first few days, hand it to AI, and the assignment practically writes itself. That's potentially 40 hours of work — bypassed.

→ TEACHING PRACTICE
This is the only part you can't fake. Stand in front of real students, deliver a real lesson, get real feedback on the spot. Uncomfortable? Yes. Valuable? Absolutely.

The irony: the 120-hour "gold standard" is losing its meaning because two of its three pillars were designed before AI existed.

Every month, thousands of CELTA-certified teachers enter the profession. Some did the hard work. Some didn't. Employers can't tell the difference.

My proposed fix is simple:
— Replace video observations with 20-minute peer mock-teaching sessions (rotating, live, impossible to outsource)
— Replace 1,000-word essays with short 300-word plans + a live peer presentation and defence

Same 120 hours. Dramatically more learning. Zero shortcuts available.

Cambridge built a great filter. It's just time to patch the holes.

Have you done CELTA or a similar certification? Curious what your experience was like. 👇
