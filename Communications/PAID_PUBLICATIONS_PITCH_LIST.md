# Paid Publications — Pitch List
## Mental Health / Neurodiversity / Personal Essay / Education

Last updated: 2026-06-03

## Tier 1 — Pays, Matches Your Voice Exactly

### Headspace Magazine
- **Pays:** $150 personal essay / $200 reported+personal / $300 fully reported
- **Email:** submissions@headspace.com
- **What they want:** Mental health first-person essays. "A Day With ____" series fits perfectly.
- **Your angle:** "A Day With Bipolar I and ADHD in La Réunion" — multilingual, diaspora, father

### OC87 Recovery Diaries
- **Pays:** Yes (rates on request)
- **URL:** [oc87recoverydiaries.org](https://oc87recoverydiaries.org)
- **What they want:** Mental health recovery stories — hope, honesty, lived experience
- **Your angle:** The Forensic Auditor essay or the generational trauma piece

### The Mighty
- **Pays:** $50-100 per essay
- **URL:** [themighty.com/submit](https://themighty.com/contribute)
- **What they want:** Disability, mental health, chronic illness lived experience
- **Your angle:** Bipolar + ADHD + multilingual = unusual and valuable voice

### ADDitude Magazine
- **Pays:** Yes (contact for rates)
- **URL:** [additudemag.com/write-for-us](https://www.additudemag.com/write-for-us/)
- **Email:** letters@additudemag.com
- **What they want:** First-person ADHD experience, especially adults, especially late-diagnosed
- **Your angle:** "I Was Diagnosed at 40: What ADHD Looks Like When You Speak Five Languages"

### Mad in America
- **Pays:** $0 but massive reach (500k readers), builds authority fast
- **URL:** [madinamerica.com/submissions](https://www.madinamerica.com/submissions/)
- **What they want:** Critical psychiatry, lived experience of mental health system, advocacy
- **Your angle:** CELTA forensic audit piece — institutional gaslighting of a disabled person

## Tier 2 — Strong Reach, Less Pay

### Medium (Partner Program)
- **Pays:** Per-read revenue share once in Partner Program (requires 100 followers first)
- **URL:** [medium.com](https://medium.com)
- **Best publications to submit to:**
  - Invisible Illness (mental health, huge)
  - An Injustice! (advocacy)
  - The Bilingual Writer (language/identity)
  - ADHD Essentials
- **Strategy:** Post first 3 essays directly. Build 100 followers. Apply to Partner Program.

### Substack
- **Pays:** You control — reader subscriptions
- **URL:** [substack.com](https://substack.com)
- **Strategy:** Start free newsletter. 3 posts/month minimum. Convert to paid after 200 subscribers.
- **Newsletter name idea:** "The Broken Compass" — mental health, language, survival

### Psych Central (Healthline Media)
- **Pays:** ~$75-150 per article
- **URL:** [psychcentral.com/become-a-contributor](https://psychcentral.com/become-a-contributor/)
- **What they want:** Mental health how-to with personal experience + evidence base
- **Your angle:** Bipolar productivity piece is perfect format for them

### NAMI (National Alliance on Mental Illness)
- **Pays:** No payment but enormous credibility / audience
- **URL:** [nami.org/blogs](https://www.nami.org/Blogs)
- **What they want:** Lived experience recovery stories, advocacy
- **Your angle:** Generational trauma + being a father + bipolar

## Tier 3 — Exposure and Network Building

### The Guardian (Education + Mental Health sections)
- **Pays:** £150-250 per piece
- **URL:** Pitch to: education.desk@theguardian.com / mental_health@theguardian.com
- **What they want:** Systemic criticism with personal evidence — your CELTA audit is perfect
- **Your angle:** "How a Cambridge teacher-training course failed a disabled candidate"

### Times Educational Supplement (TES)
- **Pays:** £150+
- **URL:** [tes.com/magazine](https://www.tes.com/magazine)
- **What they want:** Education system critique, teacher training, SEND in education
- **Your angle:** Neurodivergent student/trainee in a rigid certification system

### The Conversation
- **Pays:** No — but academic-friendly, high Google ranking
- **URL:** [theconversation.com/become-an-author](https://theconversation.com/global/become-an-author)
- **What they want:** Research-backed personal essays, academic angle
- **Your angle:** Your autoethnography framing of the CELTA case is literally academic
