# Daily Living Strategy — Sourov Deb

**Scope.** This is the *living* layer, not the *earning* layer. The Productivity, Health & Income Plan (29 May 2026) already governs work, money, and the mood-routed workday. This document governs everything else that keeps a day standing up: executive function, self-maintenance, admin, early warning, and rest. The two are designed to interlock — this one is the floor the income engine stands on.

**Ground rules.** Built on the documented medical record (bipolar I, ADHD, depression, clinical insomnia) and the same evidence base already cited in the income plan (Kleitman ultradian rhythm; Frank social rhythm therapy; Dodson interest-based attention; behavioural activation). Nothing here invents a clinical instruction. Dosing, diet specifics, and treatment decisions stay with Dr. Pauvert and the monthly psychiatric review.

---

## Principle: the system carries the day the person can't

The condition guarantees uneven days. A strategy that depends on willpower fails on a crash day — exactly when it's needed most. So every routine below is built to run on the *worst* version of you, not the best. If a step needs motivation to execute, it's designed wrong.

Three layers, in priority order:

1. **Anchors** — fixed points that never move (wake time, meds, one meal, one capture). These regulate mood independent of energy.
2. **Scaffolds** — external systems that replace the executive function you can't rely on (one board, one inbox, timers, alarms).
3. **Routing** — match the task to the state, never force the wrong task on the wrong day.

---

## Part 1 — The Four Anchors (non-negotiable, every day, any state)

These are the floor. On a crash day, *only* these. They are the strongest mood regulators you have and cost almost no energy.

| Anchor | Action | Why it holds |
|---|---|---|
| **Fixed wake** | Same wake time, 7 days a week, alarm out of reach | Strongest single regulator of bipolar mood (social rhythm therapy). More important than total sleep hours. |
| **Meds + water + light** | On waking: Concerta, water, daylight on the eyes | Anchors circadian rhythm and the day's chemistry before any decision is required |
| **One real meal** | One fixed, non-negotiable meal at a roughly fixed time | A meal time is a *rhythm cue*, not just food. Skipping destabilises both blood sugar and the day's structure |
| **One-line capture** | "Today I am ___ . Today the one thing is ___ ." | Names the state (so it can be routed) and sets a single anchor task. Written, not thought |

**Crash-day rule:** if all four anchors happen, the day counts. Nothing else is required. This is not lowering the bar — it's defending the rhythm that prevents the next episode.

---

## Part 2 — The External Brain (scaffolds, set up once)

ADHD + depression means working memory and task initiation cannot be trusted. Do not store anything important in your head. Store it in the system. Decide the system *once*, on a stable day, then stop redesigning it.

- **One board, one place.** A single capture point for every task, appointment, and idea (Obsidian/Logseq fits your .md workflow; a paper board works too). The rule that matters: *one* place, not five apps.
- **One capture inbox.** Anything that enters your head goes here immediately, unsorted. Sort later, in a stable window — never at the moment of capture.
- **Two alarms that fire the routine for you.** Meds prompt and wind-down prompt. On Linux, `systemd --user` timers; on phone, OS alarms. The machine remembers so you don't have to.
- **Timer for every block.** 25/5 or 90-minute ultradian blocks. The timer is the boss, not your sense of time (which the condition distorts).
- **Visible, not searchable.** Today's anchor task and next appointment must be *physically visible* — a card, a sticky, a single screen. Out of sight is out of existence.

> Design rule: if a scaffold requires you to remember to use it, it has already failed. It must be in the path, automatic, or visible by default.

## Part 3 — Self-Maintenance Routed by State

Hygiene, food, household, and movement are the first things to collapse in low states and the first to overheat in high ones. Route them like income work.

| State | Self-maintenance target | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Elevated / hypomanic** | Cap the burst: one cleaning task, then stop. Pre-cook and freeze meals for the coming low | Use the energy to *stock the larder for the crash* — but set a hard stop to protect sleep |
| **Stable / level** | Full routine: shower, real meals, one household reset, a walk | This is when you build the buffer. Batch errands into one trip |
| **Flat / low** | Minimum viable self-care: shower OR change clothes, eat from the freezer stock, one surface cleared | "Minimum viable" is a win, not a failure. The freezer stock from the high day pays off here |
| **Crash / shutdown** | Anchors only. Eat anything. No standards | Survival mode is legitimate. The pre-cooked meals and a low bar are the plan working as designed |

**The larder principle:** the single highest-leverage household move is cooking in bulk on good days so that eating on bad days requires zero executive function. This is the food equivalent of Tier-3 income — built high, consumed low.

No calorie targets, no exercise quotas here by design — rigid numbers break on a crash day and the strategy is built to survive crash days. Movement = a walk in the stable/low windows; that's the whole instruction.

## Part 4 — Admin & Appointments (the paperwork that unlocks everything)

Administrative tasks are the worst fit for an ADHD brain and the highest-stakes part of daily living right now, because the medical/disability paperwork unlocks money and care. Treat admin as a *scheduled batch*, never as something done "when I get to it" (you won't).

**The live unlocks** (track each to "done", with the next date visible):

- **ALD via Dr. Pauvert** — frees the cost of psychiatric care. Fastest financial win.
- **MDPH / RQTH** — unlocks AGEFIPH funding + employment protection + auto-entrepreneur support.
- **Sleep study (polysomnography)** — referred; chase it. Untreated apnea sabotages mood, focus, everything.
- **UPT trauma referral** — pending; book the moment the letter arrives.
- **Monthly psychiatric review** — the mood-stabiliser decision lives here. Never skip.
- **Ofqual complaint SJ3XP35D** — filed 24 May 2026; log every response and deadline.

**Admin rules:**
1. **One admin block per week**, in a stable window, timer on. Not daily — batching beats drip (context-switching cost).
2. **One task per block is a pass.** Booking a single appointment counts.
3. **Every appointment goes on the board the second it's made** — never held in memory.
4. **Phone calls are the hardest admin** — pre-write what you'll say in the capture inbox first, then dial. Script removes the initiation barrier.

## Part 5 — Early-Warning System (catch the swing before it lands)

Bipolar episodes are preceded by detectable signals. The one-line daily mood read (Part 1) is the sensor. The point is not to diagnose yourself — it's to *act early* and *bring data to your psychiatrist*.

Watch the rhythm, not the mood directly. The rhythm breaks before the mood does:

- **Sleep change** — needing much less sleep, or much more, two nights running
- **Speed change** — talking, spending, messaging, or planning faster than usual
- **Withdrawal** — cancelling, not replying, not leaving the house
- **Anchor slippage** — wake time drifting, meals skipped, capture stopped

**The protocol:**
- Log the daily one-liner (Daylio/eMoods/Bearable, or paper) so trends are *visible*, not felt.
- If two or more warning signs appear together → **hold all big decisions** (no cold-pitching, no client commitments, no quitting, no all-nighters — the "Never on a crash day" list from the income plan) and **flag it at or before the next psychiatric review.**
- The tracker chart is the single most useful thing you can hand Dr. Pauvert. It turns "I felt off" into data.

This is a self-monitoring aid, not a substitute for clinical care. The decisions stay with your psychiatrist.

---

## Part 6 — Sleep (the keystone)

Everything above depends on this. From your clinical ISI score, treated as the highest-priority routine you run.

- **Fixed wake time** — already an anchor; it matters more than bedtime.
- **Screens off ~1h pre-bed**; hydroxyzine as prescribed.
- **Wind-down alarm**, not just a wake alarm — the system tells you to stop.
- **No new launches at night** — no big decisions, no starting projects, no doom-scrolling the complaint file.
- **Push the sleep study.** It's on the admin list because it's that important.

---

## Part 7 — Connection (a real need, with a real boundary)

Isolation feeds depression and removes the external check that catches early warning signs. Daily living needs at least a thread of human contact — and it should be *human*. An AI, a journal, or a tracker is a tool, not a relationship; none of them can replace a person who notices when your wake time has drifted.

- One low-cost contact thread in the day (a message, a call, a fixed weekly meet) — built into the stable/low routine, not left to mood.
- The Mission Locale / CIDFF adviser appointments (admin list) double as human contact *and* unlock benefits.
- If a week shows withdrawal as a warning sign, contact is the *first* thing to restore, not the last.

## Print & Pin — The Daily Living Card

Cut out. Put it where you start the day, next to the income card.

```
WAKE        Same time, every day · Meds · Water · Light
NAME IT     Today I am ______ · The one thing is ______
EAT         One real meal, fixed time (freezer stock if low)
EXTERNAL    Board check · Next appointment visible · Capture inbox
ROUTE       High → cap & stock · Stable → full routine & buffer
            Low → minimum viable self-care · Crash → anchors only
WATCH       Sleep / speed / withdrawal / anchor slip — 2+ = hold decisions, flag it
NIGHT       Wind-down alarm · Screens off 1h · No launches · No doom-reading the case file
WIN TEST    Four anchors done = the day counts. Always.
```

**The whole strategy in one line:** keep the four anchors no matter what, store everything outside your head, match self-care to your state, batch the admin that unlocks your money and care, watch the rhythm for early warning, and protect sleep above all — so the bad days cost a day, not a month.
