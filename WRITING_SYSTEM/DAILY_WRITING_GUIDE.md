# Daily Writing System
**Adapted for Bipolar I + ADHD — Built on evidence-based practice**

---

## The One Rule

Write before you check anything.

Email, news, social media — these activate the comparison brain and the anxiety brain before the creative brain gets a chance. Write first. Check second. Every stable day, no exceptions.

---

## Minimum Viable System

**When**: 30–60 minutes, morning, same time every day
**Where**: Cold Turkey Writer or any tool that removes distractions
**Target**: 500 words — not good words, just words
**Topic**: Anything. If blocked, write about why you are blocked

---

## Topic Rotation (10 categories, never run out)

1. One specific memory — not the whole arc, just one scene
2. Something you learned this week — one insight, explained simply
3. A tool or resource — what it does, what problem it solves, honest verdict
4. An argument you have with yourself — both sides, no resolution required
5. A person who taught you something — unnamed if sensitive
6. Your condition, plainly — what it is like to live it, not what it is
7. A response to something you read — agree, disagree, add to it
8. A letter to your younger self — specific age, specific situation
9. What you wish someone had told you — one thing, for the person who needs it
10. What everyone in your field gets wrong — your real opinion

Rule: never repeat the same category two days in a row.

## The Bipolar Layer

| Mood State | Writing Target | Rule |
|------------|---------------|------|
| Stable | 500 words | Full session, publish or schedule |
| Low | 100 words minimum | Something exists. That is enough. |
| Rising (possible episode) | Write, stop at normal time | Do NOT extend. Protect the rhythm. |
| Crisis | One sentence, dated | You showed up. |

**The rising-episode trap**: It feels like your best work. It might be. Stop anyway. Riding an episode to produce more output extends the episode and shortens sleep. Extended episodes cost weeks.

---

## The ADHD Layer

- Use a 25-minute timer (Pomodoro — 25 on, 5 off)
- Write in one document, not multiple tabs
- Turn off internet completely during the writing window
- Keep a "parking lot" note open for stray ideas — park them, do not chase them
- Publish the imperfect version — done beats perfect every time

## Philosophy

This is not a recovery narrative. Recovery implies returning to a before that was worth returning to.

This is a navigation narrative. You are not getting back to somewhere. You are going forward to somewhere new. The compass is in your hand now.

Write that story. Someone is waiting for exactly it.
