# Free Tools I Actually Use as a Neurodivergent Writer

I have ADHD. I have bipolar I. I write every day.

Here is what makes that possible — all of it free, all of it open-source or genuinely free-tier, none of it a subscription I will forget about and resent in three months.

## Obsidian — for the words themselves

Obsidian is a local-first markdown editor. Nothing lives in a cloud someone else controls. Every file is plain text that will open on any computer in any decade.

The feature that matters for ADHD is backlinks. I write “this connects to the CELTA situation” anywhere, and later I see all the related notes without needing a filing system I will not maintain. Connections emerge instead of requiring me to impose structure.

Free. Desktop. No account needed. [obsidian.md](https://obsidian.md)

## Cold Turkey Writer — for focus

You open it. It locks your screen. Nothing works until you hit your word count or time target. No notifications. No browser. No “I’ll just check one thing.”

This sounds extreme. For an ADHD brain it is a gift. The constraint removes the decision. You are writing now. There is no alternative.

Free version is fully functional. [getcoldturkey.com](https://getcoldturkey.com)

## JobSpy — for job hunting

An open-source Python library that scrapes Indeed, LinkedIn, Glassdoor, and ZipRecruiter simultaneously with one command. Set your keyword and location. Get a CSV. Open it in a spreadsheet and work through it.

This does in five minutes what takes two hours of browser tabs.

```bash
pip install jobspy
```

[github.com/Bunsly/JobSpy](https://github.com/Bunsly/JobSpy)

## Google Apps Script — for email

I have a script in my repository that drafts targeted job-application emails in bulk. Feed it a contact list and a template with merge fields. It creates Gmail drafts — not sends, drafts — which you review before anything goes out.

This is the safest form of email automation. You stay in control. Nothing leaves without your eyes on it.

## LanguageTool — for editing

Free grammar and style checker that works in multiple languages. The browser extension catches errors in real time. The French catches my French. The English catches my English.

Useful for anyone writing in a language that is not their first, or for anyone whose ADHD brain misses the obvious.

[languagetool.org](https://languagetool.org)

## Canva free tier — for design

Not purely open-source but the free tier is complete for blog headers, social cards, and thumbnails. The AI background remover in the free tier is unreasonably good for a zero-cost tool.

[canva.com](https://canva.com)

## The actual system

1. Fixed time: 30 minutes, Cold Turkey Writer locked
2. 500 words minimum, no internet
3. Draft saved to Obsidian
4. Revise later with LanguageTool
5. Design header in Canva
6. Publish

Fancy tools do not fix a broken habit. The right tool removes enough friction that the habit has a chance.

Start with one. Use it until it bores you. Then add the next.
