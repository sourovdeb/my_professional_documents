# 1 — Exposure: where to write so you actually get read

Goal: your story and your thinking reach people who can hire you, collaborate
with you, or simply be changed by it. Quality over quantity — one good piece on
the right platform beats ten shouted into the void.

## Pick a "home base" + 2 "amplifiers"

Own your home base; rent the amplifiers. If a platform dies, you keep readers.

**Home base (you own it):**
- **Your WordPress blog.** You already have it. This is the canonical version of
  every piece. Everything else points back here. Add an email signup (free:
  Jetpack/MailPoet) so readers become *yours*, not the platform's.

**Amplifiers (free reach, but you don't own the audience):**
- **Medium** — built-in readers, good for essays on learning, languages, mental
  health, neurodiversity. Submit to publications to multiply reach.
- **Substack** — best for building a loyal email list with zero cost. Ideal for
  a weekly version of your daily writing. You own the email list (exportable).
- **LinkedIn articles/posts** — where recruiters and schools actually are. This
  is your job-and-contacts channel. Post your CELTA/teaching/ESL insights here.
- **dev.to / Hashnode** — only if you write about your automation tools/scripts.

You already drafted launch posts for LinkedIn, Medium, and Substack — see
`Communications/CELTA_LinkedIn.txt`, `CELTA_Medium.txt`, `CELTA_Substack.txt`.
Reuse them. Don't start from zero.

## The "write once, publish 4×" flow (no extra writing)

1. Write the piece once in `hub/content/blog/`.
2. Publish the full version on **WordPress** (your home base).
3. **Canonical-repost** to Medium (Medium has an official "Import story" tool that
   sets the canonical link back to your blog, so Google doesn't penalise you).
4. Post a **short version + link** on LinkedIn (3–5 sentences + the hook).
5. Send the same piece to your **Substack** list weekly.

One act of writing, four surfaces. That respects your energy.

## What gets read (matched to you)

- **Teaching English, the human way** — real classroom stories, IELTS/TOEIC tips,
  Business English. This builds your professional reputation *and* finds students.
- **Learning languages as a polyglot** — method, struggle, identity.
- **Working and living with depression + ADHD** — honest, useful, not a pity
  piece. This is rare, brave, and deeply shareable. (Publish on *your* terms —
  see the privacy note in `START_HERE.md`.)
- **Migration, France, rebuilding a career** — your lived experience.

## How exposure actually compounds

- Be **consistent** before clever. Same day, same time, same place. (See guide 7.)
- **Pick one niche per quarter** so people learn what you're "the person for".
- **Reply, don't broadcast.** Comment thoughtfully on 3 posts by people in your
  field daily. That, not the algorithm, is how a small account grows.
- **One call-to-action per piece:** "reply", "subscribe", or "let's talk" — never
  all three.
