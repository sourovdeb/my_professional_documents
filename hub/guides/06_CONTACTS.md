# 6 — Contacts: jobs, collaborators, and like-minded writers

Useful contacts come from *giving first* and *following up*. Automation finds and
drafts; you send the human line. Keep everyone in one tracker so nothing is lost
on a foggy day.

## One tracker for everything
You already have contact files — recycle them, don't restart:
- `personal_doc2_extracted/MASTER_CONTACT_DIRECTORY.md`
- `personal_doc2_extracted/CONTACTS_AND_EMAILS_FOUR_CHANNELS.md`

Make one Google Sheet with these columns and let everything flow into it:

| Name | Org | Type (job/writer/partner) | Channel | First contact | Last touch | Next follow-up | Status | Notes |

`Type` keeps job leads and creative partners in one view without mixing them up.

## Who to reach, and how

**Schools & language centres (jobs):**
- Language schools, business-English providers, universities, IELTS/TOEIC test
  centres in your region. Indeed + France Travail surface these (guide 4).
- Message = one specific reason you fit *them* + your CELTA + a link to one blog
  post that shows your thinking. Short. Human. One ask: "open to a quick call?"

**Recruiters (jobs):**
- LinkedIn is where they are. Post your teaching insights weekly (guide 1) so
  they find *you*. Connect with a one-line note, never a wall of text.

**Writers & creative partners (your projects):**
- Substack Notes, Medium, dev.to, r/writing, r/TEFL. Comment usefully on 3 posts
  a day for two weeks *before* you pitch anything. Warm beats cold every time.
- Pitch = "I liked X you wrote; I'm working on Y; want to swap drafts / collab?"

## The follow-up rule (where results actually live)
- Day 0: send.
- Day 5: one short, friendly nudge. ("Bringing this back to the top of your
  inbox — no pressure.")
- Day 14: final nudge, then let it rest.
Most yeses arrive on the second message. Set the reminder so your memory doesn't
have to carry it.

## Protect your energy
- Cap outreach at **one real message a day** (the daily loop). Consistency beats
  volume and won't burn you out.
- Templates for the boring 80%; a genuine first line for the 20% that matters.
- A "no" or silence is data, not a verdict on you. Log it and move on.
