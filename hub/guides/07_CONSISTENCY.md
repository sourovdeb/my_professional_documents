# 7 — Consistency: productivity that survives ADHD and low days

Most productivity advice assumes a steady brain and a full tank. Yours isn't
always either — and that's fine. This system is built to keep working *because*
of that, not in spite of it. Your own `DAILY_LIVING_STRATEGY.md` says it well:
"You are not lazy. Your brain works differently." Build around that truth.

## The core principle: lower the bar until you can't fail
A streak you keep at 5 minutes beats a perfect plan you abandon in a week.
Define a **"minimum day"** so small it's almost embarrassing:
- Minimum day = write the **hook** (one or two lines). That's it. Streak intact.
- Good day = the full 20-minute loop in `START_HERE.md`.
Shrink the task, not the standard. Quality stays; quantity flexes with energy.

## Techniques that fit an ADHD brain
- **Timeboxing over to-do lists.** A list is infinite and shaming; a 15-minute
  box is finite and kind. Set a timer; stop when it rings.
- **Pomodoro, gentle version.** 15 on, 5 off. Two or three rounds is a full day.
- **Body-doubling.** Write alongside someone (a video call, a café, a "focus
  room" stream). Presence borrows you focus. Free and surprisingly powerful.
- **Implementation intentions.** "After my morning coffee, I open the template."
  Anchor the habit to something that already happens. Don't rely on willpower.
- **One frog, one tab.** Pick the single most important task; close everything
  else. ADHD doesn't multitask — it task-switches and bleeds energy.
- **Externalise memory.** Capture every idea/contact/follow-up into the bank or
  tracker the second it appears. Your head is for thinking, not storing.

## The weekly reset (10 minutes, Sunday)
- What did I publish / send this week? (Celebrate it — out loud.)
- What's one thing to carry forward, one to drop?
- Top up the idea bank to 5 unwritten items so Monday is frictionless.
Keep it short. A heavy review system is one more thing to abandon.

## When you fall off (you will, everyone does)
- No "starting over." You just **start again**, today, at minimum.
- Never two zero-days in a row — that's the only rule that matters.
- Missing a day is a comma, not a full stop. The system waits for you.

## Make the environment do the work
- Template ready, idea bank stocked, timer in reach, socials blocked (guide 3).
- A frictionless start is the whole game. Decisions cost spoons; remove them in
  advance so the tired version of you can still show up.
