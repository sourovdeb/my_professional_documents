# 8 — Self-care: protect the person who does all of this

None of the other guides work if you burn out. This one comes first in
importance, even though it's number 8. Treat it as infrastructure, not reward.

> This is practical, peer-level guidance for daily living — **not** medical
> advice, and never a replacement for your doctor or therapist. If you are in
> crisis, jump to the bottom of this page now.

## The non-negotiables (the floor, not the ceiling)
On any day, protect these four before any productivity:
1. **Sleep** — a steady wake time matters more than a perfect bedtime. It anchors
   mood and attention more than almost anything else.
2. **Food and water** — low fuel reads exactly like low mood and low focus. Eat
   something before you judge how the day is going.
3. **Movement** — a 10-minute walk is a clinically real antidepressant dose, not
   a cliché. Outside light especially.
4. **One human contact** — a message, a call, a "hi". Isolation deepens the dip.

## Pace, don't sprint (the spoons rule)
- You have a limited budget of energy each day. Spend it on purpose: one
  important thing, then rest *before* you're empty, not after you crash.
- "Boom and bust" — overdoing it on a good day, then collapsing — costs more than
  a steady, modest pace. Protect the floor; let the ceiling vary.

## Build tiny buffers against the hard days
- A "**bad-day kit**": the minimum-day task (guide 7), a comfort playlist, one
  number to call, one easy meal. Decide it now, while you're okay, so the foggy
  version of you doesn't have to.
- **Separate worth from output.** A quiet day is not a failed day. The job hunt
  and the dispute are stressful; your value is not their verdict.

## Manage the specific stressors you're carrying
- The **CELTA dispute** and **MDPH application** are heavy. Batch them: a fixed,
  limited window (e.g. one afternoon a week), not a constant background hum.
  Outside that window, you're allowed to put them down.
- Ask for accommodations without shame — that's what the MDPH process is *for*.
  See guide 9 for the official routes.

## Crisis resources (France) — keep these reachable
- **3114** — national suicide-prevention line, free, 24/7. https://3114.fr
- **15** (SAMU) — medical emergencies.
- **112** — European emergency number.
- **SOS Amitié** — listening line: https://www.sos-amitie.com
If you ever feel unsafe, contact one of these *first*. Everything in this repo
can wait. You can't be replaced; a blog post can.
