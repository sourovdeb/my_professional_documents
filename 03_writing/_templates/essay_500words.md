**HOOK PARAGRAPH (50–80 words)**

Set the scene or drop the reader into the moment. Use a specific detail — a day, a place, a person, a number. No generalisations yet.

---

**THE TURN (100–150 words)**

Here is where you go personal. Connect the hook to your own experience. Be specific. Dates matter. Names matter (change them if needed). Don't say "many people struggle with this" — say what *you* did, what you felt, what you got wrong.

---

**THE CORE IDEA (150–200 words)**

State the thing you actually believe. This is the argument. Not a list — one idea, developed. Use one concrete example or piece of evidence. If you reference research or external sources, link them.

Active voice. Short sentences when the idea is important. Longer sentences when you're building up.

---

**THE PRACTICAL (100 words)**

What do you do with this? What did you change, or what would you change if you could? One or two concrete actions, not a listicle.

---

**THE CLOSE (50–80 words)**

End with the emotional truth, not a summary. No "In conclusion." Bring back an image from the opening if you can — that creates a circle. Leave the reader with one question or one sentence they might say aloud.
