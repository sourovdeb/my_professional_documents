# Productivity Protocols in Psychology

## Beyond Time Management: The ADHD Paradigm

- **Pomodoro Technique**: 25-minute focused sprints with 5-minute breaks
- **Deep Work**: Cal Newport's framework for sustained concentration
- **ADHD-Specific Strategies**: Externalizing memory, visual timers, body doubling
- **Neurodiversity Advantage**: Hyperfocus, pattern recognition, creative problem-solving
- **The Cost of Context-Switching**: 20-40% productivity loss per interruption

**Speaker Notes:**
Traditional productivity advice often fails neurodivergent individuals. The case study reveals how standard protocols must be adapted. Sourov's ADHD diagnosis (May 2025) required developing personalized systems. The training center's failure to accommodate demonstrates how institutional rigidness creates barriers. Productivity isn't one-size-fits-all—it's about understanding cognitive architecture.

# Pronunciation & Phonology

## The Hidden Complexity of Language Learning

- **Phonemes vs. Graphemes**: 44 sounds in English, only 26 letters
- **Suprasegmentals**: Stress, rhythm, intonation—often overlooked in ELT
- **Interference**: Native language phonology affects second language acquisition
- **CELTA Standards**: Cambridge requires 6 hours of phonology input
- **The Case Study**: Training center's remote delivery compromised phonology instruction

**Speaker Notes:**
Sourov's 18-year ELT career and CELTA qualification highlight phonology's importance. His complaint against The ELT Hub noted that remote training (Jan-Feb 2026) lacked proper phonology components. This isn't just academic—mispronunciation affects communication, confidence, and professional opportunities. The case demonstrates how educational standards must adapt to delivery modes.

# Evolutionary Bias in Human Behavior

## Why Systems Fail the Vulnerable

- **Confirmation Bias**: We favor information confirming preexisting beliefs
- **In-Group Bias**: Preferential treatment for perceived group members
- **Status Quo Bias**: Resistance to change, even when change is needed
- **The Case Study**: Training center's failure to activate equal opportunities procedures
- **Systemic Discrimination**: ADHD and depression disclosure triggered obligation, but was ignored

**Speaker Notes:**
Evolutionary biases explain why the training center failed Sourov. Their in-group bias (favoring non-disabled candidates) and status quo bias (maintaining existing procedures) overrode legal obligations. The Equality Act 2010 and French Qualiopi Indicateur 26 require anticipatory adjustments—meaning the center should have acted BEFORE a request was made. This is evolutionary psychology meeting modern law.

# LLM Fundamentals

## How AI Understands and Generates Text

- **Transformer Architecture**: Self-attention mechanisms process words in parallel
- **Tokenization**: Breaking text into ~50,000 vocabulary units
- **Context Windows**: Limited memory (typically 4K-32K tokens)
- **Fine-Tuning**: Aligning base models with human values and tasks
- **The Case Study**: AI tools analyzed legal documents for Sourov's discrimination case

**Speaker Notes:**
Large Language Models don't "understand" like humans—they predict probability distributions. Yet they can identify patterns in legal documents that humans miss. Sourov used AI to analyze the CELTA training center's compliance failures, finding gaps in their equal opportunities procedures. This demonstrates AI's potential to democratize access to justice, especially for individuals facing institutional resistance.
