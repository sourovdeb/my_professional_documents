# Consistency System — productivity routed by mood, not willpower

*Condensed from your own `DAILY_LIVING_STRATEGY.md` and the Stability-First program. Built on the documented record: bipolar I, ADHD, depression, clinical insomnia. Your doctors keep all clinical decisions; this is structure, not treatment.*

The mistake every generic productivity system makes: it assumes a stable baseline you don't have. Your condition guarantees uneven days. So the only system that works is one that **matches the task to the state** — and that **runs on your worst day, not your best.** If a step needs motivation to fire, it's designed wrong.

---

## The one daily question

Before anything, write one line: **"Today I am ___. Today the one thing is ___."**

Naming the state lets you *route* it. Naming one thing stops the ADHD overwhelm of facing everything at once. Written, not thought — thoughts evaporate.

---

## Route the day by state

| State | What it feels like | What to do | What to NOT do |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Crash** | Heavy, foggy, can't start | The four anchors only (see `08_SELF_CARE/`). Three sentences of capture. Day counts. | Don't decide anything important. Don't quit anything. Don't judge yourself. |
| **Low-steady** | Flat but functional | One 500-word draft + one admin task. Routine work, low novelty. | Don't start big new projects. |
| **Good-steady** | Clear, energy present | **This is your stable day.** Edit drafts, schedule posts, run the weekly automation batch, empty the idea inbox, reach one collaborator. | Don't over-commit future-you. |
| **High / surge** | Fast, confident, "I'll do everything" | Channel it into *creating* (drafting, building) — but **no irreversible decisions**: no big spends, no burning bridges, no sending the angry email. Sleep on anything that costs money or relationships. | Don't trust the certainty. The surge lies about consequences. |

> The bipolar trap is the surge: it spends the rent and writes the email you regret. Rule: **anything irreversible waits 24 hours and one night's sleep.** Always.

## The ADHD scaffolds (set up once, then trust them)

- **One board, one inbox.** Every task and idea lands in *one* place (Obsidian/Logseq or paper). Not five apps. The discipline is *one*.
- **The timer is the boss.** 25/5 or 90-minute blocks. Your sense of time is unreliable; the timer isn't.
- **Visible, not searchable.** Today's one thing must be physically in sight — a card, a sticky, one screen. Out of sight is out of existence.
- **Two alarms fire the routine for you:** meds prompt, wind-down prompt. The machine remembers so you don't.
- **Body-double / co-working.** Working alongside someone (even a video call) makes starting possible. Use it on stall days.

## Beat "productive avoidance" (your named pattern)

You reorganise, research, and polish to avoid *finishing*. Counters:

1. **Define "done" before you start.** "Done = 500 words saved," not "done = perfect."
2. **Ship at 80%.** The last 20% is where avoidance hides. Publish; iterate later.
3. **Stop mid-sentence.** Easy restart tomorrow beats a clean stop you dread returning to.
4. **One thing, not the list.** The list is for the board, not for now.

---

## The weekly review (15 minutes, stable day only)

- What got published? (the only output that matters)
- What's in the idea inbox to promote or delete?
- One automation batch run? One collaborator contacted?
- How were the moods this week — any early warning signs to flag at the next psychiatric review?

That's it. Don't redesign the system. Run it.
