# Taking Care of Yourself — the floor the whole system stands on

*From your own Stability-First program and Daily Living Strategy. This is not optional extra; it's the load-bearing wall. The writing, the jobs, the fight — none of it stands if this floor gives way.*

The principle that runs everything: **the system carries the day the person can't.** On a crash day, willpower is gone exactly when you need it most. So the floor is built to need almost no energy.

---

## The Four Anchors (non-negotiable, every day, any state)

If only these four happen, **the day counts.** This isn't lowering the bar — it's defending the rhythm that prevents the next episode.

| Anchor | Action | Why it holds |
|---|---|---|
| **Fixed wake** | Same wake time, 7 days a week. Alarm out of reach. | The single strongest regulator of bipolar mood. More important than total hours slept. |
| **Meds + water + light** | On waking: meds, water, daylight on the eyes. | Anchors your circadian rhythm and the day's chemistry *before* any decision is required. |
| **One real meal** | One fixed meal at a roughly fixed time. | A meal time is a rhythm cue, not just food. Skipping destabilises blood sugar and the day. |
| **One-line capture** | "Today I am ___. Today the one thing is ___." | Names the state so you can route it. Sets one anchor task, not a crushing list. |

> Why fixed wake and meal *times* matter so much: they're the core of **social rhythm therapy (IPSRT)** — keeping daily rhythms regular is one of the best-evidenced ways to stabilise bipolar mood. Regularity beats intensity.

## Protect sleep like it's medication (because for you, it is)

Clinical insomnia is on your record, and for bipolar, disrupted sleep can *trigger* episodes. Basics, from CBT-I:

- **Wind-down alarm**, not just a wake alarm. Screens off, lights down, warm screen (f.lux) before bed.
- **Bed is for sleep.** If you're awake 20+ minutes, get up, low light, return when sleepy.
- **No big decisions, no doomscroll, no the-angry-email after dark.** Night amplifies everything.
- Sleep problems that won't settle → flag them at your psychiatric review. Don't tough it out.

## Early-warning signs (catch the episode before it catches you)

Keep a short personal list and check it weekly. Common signals to watch (yours may differ — build your own with Dr Padovani):

- **Toward high:** less sleep but more energy, racing ideas, spending urges, certainty you can do everything, irritability.
- **Toward low:** waking dread, everything feels heavy, withdrawing, "what's the point," missing anchors.

When 2+ signs show for several days: **slow down, hold the routine harder, no irreversible decisions, and tell someone** — your wife, your psychiatrist. Naming it early is the whole skill.
