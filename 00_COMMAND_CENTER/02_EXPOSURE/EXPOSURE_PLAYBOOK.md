# Exposure Playbook — where to get read, and how

*Reach follows quality and consistency, in that order. A small true audience beats a large indifferent one.*

You don't need to "go viral." You need the right 1,000 people to find your record and trust it: people who hire writers and teachers, people fighting the same institutions, people who care about disability and ADHD and second acts. Here's where they are and how to reach them without burning yourself out.

---

## Your home base: sourovdeb.com (WordPress)

Publish here **first, always.** You own it; no platform can delete it. Everything else points back here.

- One clear "Start here" page: who you are, the through-line ("information is the democracy"), and your three best essays.
- Use categories that match your life: *Living with bipolar & ADHD · The CELTA fight · Hospitality & languages · Philosophy*.
- Install free SEO (Yoast or Rank Math) so each post has a title, meta description, and clean slug. Search is slow but compounding — it's the audience that finds you while you sleep.

---

## The distribution layer (publish home first, then adapt)

| Platform | Who's there | What works | Effort |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Medium** | General readers, "Personality/Mental Health/Disability" topics; pays via Partner Program | Your scene→lesson essays; submit to publications (*The Ascent*, *Invisible Illness*, *Human Parts*) | Low — paste + canonical link |
| **Substack** | Email subscribers who come back | A free weekly with your best essay; owning the email list is the real asset | Low–medium |
| **LinkedIn** | Recruiters, employers, English-teaching world | The reframe + the "from the inside" pieces; this is also your job pipeline | Low |
| **dev.to / Hashnode** | Builders | Only your tools/automation posts (open-source toolkit, the writing system) | Low |
| **Reddit** | Tight communities | *r/bipolar, r/ADHD, r/CPTSD, r/TEFL, r/disability* — give value, link rarely, read each sub's rules first | Medium — easy to misstep |
| **Hacker News** | Tech/indie crowd | One-off: "I built a writing system that survives a bipolar crash" + open-source repo | Spiky |

**Rule of one:** publish to WordPress, then in the same week adapt **one** essay to **one** other platform. Don't fan out to five. Depth on one channel beats noise on all.

---

## How to actually get read (the levers that work)

1. **Headlines do 80% of the work.** Specific + a promise of insight. "The Forensic Auditor" → fine for your blog; for Medium, "What I Did When an Institution Rewrote My Reality." Test both.
2. **Write for one person.** Picture the 19-year-old just diagnosed, scared, googling at 2am. Write to them. Universality comes from specificity, never the reverse.
3. **Consistency is the algorithm.** Same days each week. Every platform rewards showing up; all of them punish silence then bingeing.
4. **Reply to every comment for the first year.** Early readers who feel seen become the people who share you.
5. **Give before you ask.** Useful, honest, free. Trust is the only currency that compounds.
6. **One "anchor" long piece, many short ones.** Your autoethnography or a definitive essay is the lighthouse; the 500-word posts are the boats that bring people to it.

---

## Beyond self-publishing (slower, higher-credibility)

- **Guest posts / op-eds** on disability, mental health, and EdTech sites — pitch one tight idea, link your blog as proof you can write.
- **Podcasts** on ADHD, bipolar, trauma recovery — your story plus your forensic-auditor angle is a bookable episode. Search "[topic] podcast guest submission."
- **Local/Réunion & French press** on disability rights and the CELTA case — your documentation makes you a credible source, not just a subject.
- **Newsletters in your niche** — offer to write one piece for someone with an existing list; borrow their audience honestly.

Pitch only when you have 5–10 strong published pieces behind you. The blog is the portfolio that makes every pitch easy.

---

## What to measure (and what to ignore)

Track three numbers, monthly, no more: **posts published**, **email subscribers**, **replies/messages from real people**. Ignore vanity views. The job, the partners, and the meaning all come through *those three*, not the view counter.
