## Why learning this matters for *you* specifically

- **Self-knowledge stabilises.** "Looking at my diagnostic today, I know exactly why" — that clarity is itself protective.
- **Knowledge is leverage in your case.** France's anticipatory duty and disability protections turn on *facts about your condition*. Official sources are the only ones that carry weight with CPAM, MDPH, and Ofqual.
- **It separates you from the shame.** ADHD explains the last-minute submissions. Bipolar explains the surges and crashes. C-PTSD explains the hypervigilance. None of it is a character failing. It has names, and the names come from medicine.

## Bipolar I disorder

**Learn:** what a manic vs depressive vs mixed episode actually is; why it's lifelong and episodic; why **mood stabilisers** and routine matter; why sleep loss can trigger episodes; what early-warning signs to track.

- 🇺🇸 **NIMH — Bipolar Disorder** (clear, comprehensive): https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/publications/bipolar-disorder
- 🇬🇧 **NHS — Bipolar disorder**: https://www.nhs.uk/mental-health/conditions/bipolar-disorder/
- 🇫🇷 **HAS / ALD** — bipolar falls under **ALD n°23** (affection psychiatrique de longue durée → 100% care). See your `Communications/CONTACTS_AND_EMAILS_FOUR_CHANNELS.md` for the exact legal basis (CSS art. L324-1, R322-5) and the ready ALD request email.
- 🇫🇷 **PsyCom** (French public mental-health info): https://www.psycom.org

## ADHD (TDAH) — adult

**Learn:** that adult ADHD is real and treatable; first-line is **medication + CBT**; the interest-based attention pattern (Dodson); why structure beats willpower.

- 🇫🇷 **ameli.fr — TDAH, traitement** (methylphenidate, reimbursement): https://www.ameli.fr/assure/sante/themes/trouble-deficit-attention-hyperactivite-tdah/soins-prescrits-traitement

**Learn:** **CBT-I** is the first-line, best-evidenced treatment (not just sleeping pills); sleep regularity is part of bipolar stability.

## Complex PTSD (C-PTSD)

**Learn:** C-PTSD is now a formal **WHO ICD-11** diagnosis (distinct from PTSD), tied to prolonged, repeated trauma — which fits the childhood record. Core features: the PTSD symptoms *plus* difficulties with emotion regulation, self-worth, and relationships.

- 🌍 **WHO ICD-11 — Complex PTSD** (the official diagnostic standard): https://icd.who.int (search "complex post-traumatic stress disorder", code 6B41)
- 🇬🇧 **NHS — Complex PTSD**: https://www.nhs.uk/mental-health/conditions/post-traumatic-stress-disorder-ptsd/complex/
- Treatments to ask about: trauma-focused therapy, EMDR, phased approaches. Your file already mentions a **UPT (Unité de Psychotraumatologie)** referral — pursue it.

## Your rights in France (official, and already half-documented in this repo)

- **ALD (100% care)** — via CPAM, médecin traitant. Bipolar = ALD n°23.
- **RQTH** (worker-with-disability recognition), **AAH** (allowance), **PCH**, **CMI** — via MDPH / CDAPH.
- **AGEFIPH** — funds workplace accommodation for disabled workers.
- **Exam accommodation** — via the university *référent handicap* (Code de l'éducation).
- The exact authorities, legal articles, and ready-to-send French emails are in **`Communications/CONTACTS_AND_EMAILS_FOUR_CHANNELS.md`** and **`Biography_and_Medical/FRANCE_EU_DISABILITY_REGISTRATION_GUIDE.md`**. Use those; they're already done.

## How to use this without spiralling

- **Read one section at a time, on a steady day.** Not all at once, not on a crash day.
- **Bring questions to your appointments**, not conclusions. Official info prepares you to ask better questions — it doesn't replace your doctors.
- **Verify before you cite.** For any rights claim, confirm the current rule on the official site (.gouv.fr, ameli.fr, has-sante.fr, ofqual.gov.uk) — rules and addresses change.
