## Audio Editing & Recording

### Audacity — Industry Standard Free Audio Editor
- **URL:** https://www.audacityteam.org
- **OS:** Windows, macOS, Linux
- **Best for:** Podcast recording, voice cleanup, noise reduction
- **Low-energy tip:** Use the preset effect chain: Noise Reduction → Normalize → Export MP3. Three clicks, done.
- **Key features:** Multi-track, noise reduction, loudness normalization, MP3/WAV export, VST plugins

### Ocenaudio — Simpler Than Audacity
- **URL:** https://www.ocenaudio.com
- **OS:** Windows, macOS, Linux
- **Best for:** Quick single-file cleanup (record voice, remove noise, save)
- **Low-energy tip:** Non-destructive editing means you can never accidentally destroy your recording
- **Key features:** Real-time preview, spectrogram, handles large files without lag

### Tenacity — Privacy-Focused Audacity Fork
- **URL:** https://tenacityaudio.org
- **OS:** Windows, macOS, Linux
- **Best for:** Same as Audacity but without telemetry/data collection
- **Key features:** All Audacity features + no data tracking

### Reaper — Professional, Low Cost
- **URL:** https://www.reaper.fm
- **Cost:** $60 (perpetual license, free unlimited trial)
- **Best for:** When you want to grow into professional podcasting/music

### Free Online Alternative (Zero Install)
- **Chrome Music Lab:** https://musiclab.chromeexperiments.com (creative, fun)
- **TwistedWave Online:** https://twistedwave.com/online (simple audio editor in browser)
- **Veed.io Audio:** https://veed.io (clean UI, online audio + video)

## Video Editing

### OpenShot — Best for Beginners
- **URL:** https://www.openshot.org
- **OS:** Windows, macOS, Linux
- **Best for:** First-time video editors, simple blog video clips
- **Low-energy tip:** Drag video in → drag music in → export. That is the full workflow.
- **Key features:** Drag-and-drop, unlimited tracks, animated titles, transitions

### Kdenlive — Best Mid-Range Option
- **URL:** https://kdenlive.org
- **OS:** Windows, macOS, Linux
- **Best for:** YouTube-quality teaching videos
- **Key features:** Color correction, audio ducking, proxy clips (works on slow computers)

### Shotcut — Most Actively Maintained
- **URL:** https://shotcut.org
- **OS:** Windows, macOS, Linux
- **GitHub stars:** 14,000+
- **Best for:** Reliable, up-to-date editing without watermarks
- **Key features:** 4K support, hundreds of filters, no subscription

### DaVinci Resolve (Free Version) — Hollywood Quality, Free
- **URL:** https://www.blackmagicdesign.com/products/davinciresolve
- **OS:** Windows, macOS, Linux
- **Best for:** When you want the best possible quality — like a professional
- **Key features:** Professional color grading, Fairlight audio studio, no watermark, no time limit
- **Low-energy tip:** Use the "Cut" page (not the Edit page) — it is specifically designed for fast editing with fewer decisions

### Online Video Editors (No Install)
- **CapCut:** https://www.capcut.com — mobile-first, AI auto-subtitles, very easy
- **Veed.io:** https://veed.io — browser-based, auto-captions, clean UI
- **Clipchamp:** https://clipchamp.com — built into Windows 11, very simple

## Graphic Design & Banners

### Canva (Free Tier) — Easiest Option
- **URL:** https://www.canva.com
- **OS:** Web + mobile
- **Best for:** Blog banners, social media graphics, thumbnails
- **Low-energy tip:** Choose a template → change the text → download. Under 2 minutes.
- **Free includes:** 1000s of templates, resize, export PNG/JPG/PDF

### Penpot — Open-Source Canva Alternative
- **URL:** https://penpot.app
- **OS:** Web-based (also self-hostable)
- **Best for:** Modern UI design + banners, works in browser, no install
- **Key features:** Real-time collaboration, components, CSS Grid

### GIMP — Professional Photo/Banner Editing
- **URL:** https://www.gimp.org
- **OS:** Windows, macOS, Linux
- **Best for:** Custom blog headers, photo editing, advanced manipulation
- **Low-energy tip:** Save your banner as a template (File > Export As Template). Next time, open template → change text → export.
- **Key features:** Layers, brushes, filters, scripts, all export formats

### Inkscape — Vector Graphics (Scales to Any Size)
- **URL:** https://inkscape.org
- **OS:** Windows, macOS, Linux
- **Best for:** Logos, scalable banners (never pixelated)
- **Key features:** SVG-based, path editing, text tools, export to PNG/PDF

### Krita — Digital Art & Illustration
- **URL:** https://krita.org
- **OS:** Windows, macOS, Linux
- **Best for:** Custom blog illustrations, creative thumbnails
- **Low-energy note:** Therapeutic — drawing/painting is excellent for mental health management

### Free Banner Templates & Resources
- **Freepik:** https://www.freepik.com — free templates (attribution required on free plan)
- **Unsplash:** https://unsplash.com — completely free photos, no attribution
- **Pexels:** https://www.pexels.com — same, free commercial use
- **SVG Repo:** https://www.svgrepo.com — free SVG icons and illustrations
- **Google Fonts:** https://fonts.google.com — free professional fonts

## Complete Low-Energy Content Workflow

```
DAY 1: Write (5 minutes)
  → Open Mistral or Claude
  → Type: "Write 500 words about [teaching topic]"
  → Copy to Google Sheet → mark ready
  → System auto-publishes as draft

DAY 2: Audio (3 minutes on a good day)
  → Paste blog post into TTSMaker or ElevenLabs
  → Download MP3
  → Upload to WordPress media library
  → Add audio player shortcode to the post

DAY 3: Banner (2 minutes using template)
  → Open Canva → Your saved banner template
  → Change the title text
  → Download → upload to WordPress as featured image

RESULT: Professional blog post with audio version and custom banner
Total time per post: 10 minutes across 3 separate short sessions.
On hard days: skip audio and banner — the writing is what matters.
```
