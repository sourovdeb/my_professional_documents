# System Design for Wellbeing

> **This system was built with your mental health first. Not as an afterthought, but as the foundation.**

## The Problem We're Solving

You have bipolar II and depression. You need to:
- Build a portfolio (writing shows credibility)
- Find income (job searching is mandatory)
- Maintain consistency (without burning out)
- Take care of yourself (no heroic all-nighters)
- Keep your mind engaged (prevents spirals)
- Stay organized (reduces anxiety)

**Traditional productivity systems fail people with bipolar/depression because they assume:**
- You can work at the same pace every day (you can't)
- Motivation is consistent (it isn't)
- Willpower is unlimited (it's not)
- Rest = laziness (it doesn't)

**This system is different.**

## Design Principles

### 1. **Consistency Over Perfection**

**Old mindset**: "I need to write 7 essays/week to be productive"
**New mindset**: "4-5 essays/week is sustainable. Some weeks I write 10. Some weeks I write 1. That's OK."

Why?
- Bipolar cycles = energy fluctuations are *normal*
- Depression = some weeks you survive, you don't thrive
- Burnout makes everything worse
- Slow + steady > fast + crash

**Math**: 4 essays/week × 52 weeks = 208 essays/year (a real body of work)

---

### 2. **Decision Fatigue Elimination**

**Old problem**: "What should I write about?"

Solution: **18+ prompts ready to go.** Every prompt:
- Has a research source (no "where do I find information?" paralysis)
- Is tied to your experience (not generic)
- Includes WHY it matters
- Takes 5 minutes to pick

**No more blank-page paralysis.**

---

### 3. **Mental State Awareness**

Every essay template includes:

```
## Wellness check: Am I writing from a good mental state?
☐ Yes ☐ No
```

**Permission to skip.** If you're struggling:
- Don't force an essay
- Do something gentler (pick a lighter prompt, write 250 words, update your contacts file)
- Consistency means "showing up," not "performing perfectly"

---

### 4. **Sustainable Pace = Weekly Rhythm**

**Monday-Wednesday**: Write essays (low-pressure)
**Thursday**: Validate + publish
**Friday**: Update tracking (Google Sheet)
**Saturday-Sunday**: Rest (seriously, rest)

This means:
- 3-4 writing days/week (not 7)
- Batching work (more efficient)
- Built-in rest days (prevents burnout)
- Weekly reset (keeps you grounded)

---

### 5. **Automation Handles the Boring**

**What YOU do**: Write (your superpower)
**What SCRIPTS do**: Find jobs, validate essays, track progress

Why?
- Executive function is finite (especially with depression)
- Depression makes repetitive tasks feel impossible
- Automation = you conserve energy for creative work
- Scripts run while you sleep (job searching happens at 8 AM daily)

---

### 6. **No Credentials in the Repo**

**Problem**: Credentials in plaintext = anxiety about security leaks

**Solution**:
- WordPress helper generates instructions (no direct FTP)
- You copy-paste into WordPress
- Job search is manual (respects robots.txt)
- No sensitive data exposed

**Result**: You can share this repo, collaborate, submit for review = no security guilt

---

### 7. **Visual Progress Tracking**

**Google Sheet shows**:
- Essays written this week
- Essays published this month
- Cumulative total
- Consistency patterns

**Why it helps**:
- Depression lies ("you haven't done anything")
- Data tells the truth ("you wrote 5 essays this week")
- Progress is visible = motivation
- Patterns show your cycles (good for self-understanding)

## Mental Health Check-In System

### Daily

Before you write, ask:

```
☐ Did I sleep OK last night?
☐ Am I in a stable mental state right now?
☐ Do I have 30-60 minutes of focus energy?
☐ Is writing what I need today?
```

If all YES → Write
If any NO → Do something else (read prompts, update contacts, rest)

---

### Weekly

Friday check-in:

```
☐ Did I write 3-5 essays this week?
☐ Are they in my drafts folder?
☐ Did I publish at least 1?
☐ Did I update the Google Sheet?
☐ Did I take rest days?
```

This is not a failure test. It's a **wellness check**.

If you wrote 2 essays on a rough week: **Celebrate.** That's success.

---

### Monthly

Review your patterns:

```
This month:
- Essays written: ___
- Published: ___
- Job search results reviewed: ___
- Mental health trend: ↑ Stable ↓ Struggling ↔ Cycling
- What helped: ___
- What didn't: ___
- What I need next month: ___
```

Use this to adjust. If you're struggling more:
- Write fewer essays (not zero, just fewer)
- Pick lighter prompts
- Add extra rest days
- Check in with therapy/support

## Hard Days Protocol

### When You're in a Depressive Episode

**Don't try to:** Write 5 essays, search 50 jobs, rebuild your CV

**Do instead:**
- ☐ Pick ONE light prompt (philosophy, personal story)
- ☐ Write 250 words (not 500)
- ☐ Mark it as "draft" (don't publish today)
- ☐ Rest for the rest of the day
- ☐ Commit to trying again tomorrow

**Why?**
- You maintain consistency (even small)
- You don't crash harder
- You prove to yourself you CAN keep going
- Tomorrow is a new day

---

### When You're Hypomanic

**You feel**: Like you can write 20 essays, apply to 100 jobs, rebuild everything

**Reality check:**
- You CAN write more (this is good)
- You might burn out after
- Lock in sustainable habits

**Do instead:**
- ☐ Write as much as you want (channel the energy)
- ☐ Schedule publishing later (not all at once)
- ☐ Write down ideas for future use
- ☐ Don't make permanent decisions when manic (no major repo changes)
- ☐ Sleep happens (set a bedtime alarm)

**Why?**
- Channel the energy productively
- Don't crash immediately after
- Build buffer essays for lean weeks
- Use hypomania as gift, not liability

---

### When You're Cycling (Unpredictable)

**The week feels:** Up-down-up-down, confusing, exhausting

**Reality check:**
- This is bipolar, not failure
- Consistency means "showing up despite it"

**Do instead:**
- ☐ Lower your target to 2 essays/week (not 4-5)
- ☐ Pick prompts about bipolar/mental health (therapizing through writing)
- ☐ Check in daily (is today an up day or down day?)
- ☐ Job searching: minimal (just check saved searches, don't do heavy lifting)
- ☐ Publish less, draft more (get ideas out, polish later)

**Why?**
- You're still building
- Cycling is normal, not a bug
- Writing helps you understand it
- Reduced pressure = fewer crashes

## What Counts as Success

### A Good Week
- You wrote something (1-5 essays)
- You didn't hurt yourself
- You slept (even if not great)
- You ate (most days)
- You reached out to 1 person (or talked to therapist)

### A Good Month
- 8-20 essays written
- 3-8 published
- You tracked it (Google Sheet)
- You had at least 1 good week
- You asked for help when needed

### A Good Year
- 100+ essays written
- Visible body of work (portfolio)
- Job applications sent
- Credibility built
- Still here, still fighting, still growing

❌ "Normal" = ignoring your cycles
❌ "Healthy" = pushing through when you're drowning
❌ "Strong" = not asking for help

## Red Flags (When to Pause)

If you notice:
- [ ] You haven't slept in 3+ days
- [ ] You're writing at 4 AM for 12 hours straight
- [ ] You're skipping meals
- [ ] You feel hopeless about everything
- [ ] You're thinking about self-harm
- [ ] You've abandoned all non-writing activities

**Stop. Tell someone. Call therapist/crisis line.**
