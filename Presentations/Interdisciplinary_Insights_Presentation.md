## Slide 2: Psychology - The Science of Getting Things Done

### **Productivity Protocols: How the Brain Manages Complexity**

- **Cognitive Load Theory**: Working memory has limited capacity (4-7 items)
- **Chunking**: Grouping information to reduce cognitive load
- **Interleaving**: Mixing different topics for better retention
- **Spaced Repetition**: Timing reviews based on forgetting curves
- **Pomodoro Technique**: 25-minute focused bursts with 5-minute breaks

**Key Insight:** The brain is not a computer; it's a prediction machine that thrives on patterns and rest.

**Speaker Notes:**
The human brain processes approximately 11 million bits per second, but only 40 bits reach conscious awareness. This bottleneck explains why productivity protocols work: they align with our cognitive architecture. Cognitive Load Theory, developed by John Sweller, shows that when working memory is overwhelmed, learning collapses. Chunking, popularized by George Miller's "magical number seven," helps us manage this by creating meaningful groups.

## Slide 3: Psychology - The Hidden Hand of Evolution

### **Evolutionary Bias: Why We're Not as Rational as We Think**

- **Confirmation Bias**: Seeking information that confirms pre-existing beliefs
- **Loss Aversion**: Fear of losses is twice as powerful as hope of gains (Kahneman & Tversky)
- **Dunning-Kruger Effect**: The less we know, the more confident we are
- **Tribality**: Our brains evolved for tribal life, not modern complexity
- **Status Seeking**: Evolutionary drive for social hierarchy affects modern decisions

**Key Insight:** Our brains evolved for survival on the savannah, not for making optimal decisions in the modern world.

**Speaker Notes:**
Evolutionary psychology reveals that many of our cognitive biases served adaptive purposes. Loss aversion, for example, kept our ancestors alive. The Dunning-Kruger effect emerges because incompetent people lack the metacognition to recognize their incompetence. These biases aren't bugs; they're features of a brain optimized for a different environment.

## Slide 4: Linguistics - The Music of Speech

### **Pronunciation & Phonology: The Sound System of Language**

- **Phonemes**: Smallest sound units that distinguish meaning
- **Allophones**: Variants of phonemes that don't change meaning
- **Prosody**: The melody of speech—stress, rhythm, intonation
- **Coarticulation**: Sounds overlap and influence each other
- **Minimal Pairs**: Words that differ by only one phoneme (ship vs sheep)

**Key Insight:** Language is as much about the ear as the mouth; phonology is the interface between thought and sound.

**Speaker Notes:**
Phonology is the study of how sounds function in language. Every language has its own inventory of phonemes—English has about 44. The International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA) gives us a way to represent these sounds precisely. Prosody carries enormous meaning.

## Slide 5: Linguistics - Teaching the World's Lingua Franca

### **ELT: The Challenges and Triumphs of English Language Teaching**

- **Communicative Approach**: Focus on communication over grammar rules
- **Task-Based Learning**: Real-world tasks drive language acquisition
- **CELTA Certification**: The gold standard for English teachers (Cambridge)
- **Accessibility Challenges**: Accommodating diverse learning needs and disabilities
- **Digital Transformation**: Technology's role in language education

**Key Insight:** Teaching a language is teaching a new way of thinking, not just new vocabulary.

**Speaker Notes:**
English Language Teaching has evolved from grammar-translation methods to communicative approaches. The CELTA is one of the most respected qualifications. However, the field faces significant accessibility challenges.

## Slide 6: Artificial Intelligence - The New Frontier

### **LLM Fundamentals: How Large Language Models Work**

- **Transformer Architecture**: Self-attention mechanisms process sequences in parallel
- **Pre-training**: Learning patterns from vast amounts of text (billions of tokens)
- **Fine-tuning**: Adapting to specific tasks with labeled data
- **Emergent Abilities**: Unexpected capabilities from scale (reasoning, coding, creativity)
- **Limitations**: Hallucinations, bias, lack of true understanding

**Key Insight:** LLMs don't understand language; they model the statistical patterns of human text with astonishing accuracy.

**Speaker Notes:**
Large Language Models represent a paradigm shift in AI. The transformer architecture allows models to weigh the importance of different words dynamically. Pre-training on massive datasets teaches the model the statistical regularities of language. However, these models have no true understanding—they're sophisticated pattern completion machines.

## Slide 7: Physics - Reality at Its Most Fundamental

### **Quantum Mechanics: The Weird and Wonderful**

- **Wave-Particle Duality**: Particles exhibit both wave-like and particle-like properties
- **Superposition**: Quantum systems exist in multiple states simultaneously
- **Entanglement**: Particles remain connected across vast distances
- **Uncertainty Principle**: We cannot precisely know both position and momentum
- **Quantum Tunneling**: Particles can pass through energy barriers

**Key Insight:** At the quantum level, reality is probabilistic, not deterministic.

**Speaker Notes:**
Quantum mechanics challenges our classical intuitions. The double-slit experiment demonstrates wave-particle duality. Superposition is a fundamental property. Entanglement shows that measuring one particle instantaneously determines the state of its entangled partner.

## Slide 8: Interdisciplinary Connections - Quantum Decision Theory

### **When Physics Meets Psychology: Quantum Models of Cognition**

- **Quantum Probability**: Human decisions may follow quantum rather than classical probability
- **Superposition of Beliefs**: People can hold conflicting beliefs simultaneously
- **Interference Effects**: Context affects decisions in ways classical models can't explain
- **Entanglement of Concepts**: Some ideas are fundamentally interconnected
- **Applications**: Better decision-making models, improved AI, enhanced understanding of creativity

**Key Insight:** The same mathematical frameworks that describe quantum particles may explain human cognition.

**Speaker Notes:**
Quantum decision theory applies quantum probability to human cognition. Research shows that people's preferences can violate the axioms of classical probability theory. This doesn't mean our brains are quantum computers, but that quantum probability provides a better mathematical model for certain cognitive phenomena.
