# Legal Review Process for Microblog Posts

All migration law posts must be reviewed by a qualified immigration lawyer before publication to ensure accuracy and manage liability risk.

---

## Review Checklist for Posts

### Pre-Review (Editor)
- [ ] Draft is complete and ready for legal eyes
- [ ] All sources are cited and verified (use sources.md)
- [ ] Post includes required disclaimer: "This is informational only, not legal advice"
- [ ] Plain language confirmed (avoid jargon)
- [ ] Links to legal aid / NGO support included
- [ ] Practical checklist or action items included
- [ ] Post is 250–400 words (or note if longer)

### What Lawyer Reviews

1. **Accuracy**
   - [ ] All facts about the law are correct
   - [ ] Timelines and procedures match official sources
   - [ ] Eligibility criteria are stated accurately
   - [ ] Recent changes are correctly described
   - [ ] No outdated information

2. **Completeness**
   - [ ] No material omissions that could mislead readers
   - [ ] Exceptions or conditions are noted
   - [ ] Affected groups are clearly identified
   - [ ] Timeframes are specified (e.g., "within 30 days")

3. **Tone & Language**
   - [ ] No advice (suggestions are prefaced with "consider" or "you may want to")
   - [ ] Distinction between mandatory and optional steps is clear
   - [ ] Language is neutral, not persuasive or opinion-based
   - [ ] Appropriate level of detail for target audience

4. **Liability Risk**
   - [ ] Disclaimer is clear and prominent
   - [ ] No encouragement to bypass proper channels or legal advice
   - [ ] No statements that could be interpreted as legal advice
   - [ ] Appropriate cautions where procedures are complex
   - [ ] Legal aid contact info is included

5. **Compliance**
   - [ ] Post does not violate any applicable laws
   - [ ] Sources are reliable and official
   - [ ] No unsubstantiated claims
   - [ ] Respects privacy (no personal details of real cases)

### Lawyer's Response

The lawyer should return:
- **Approved** — post may be published as-is
- **Approved with minor edits** — suggested changes (with tracked changes or comments)
- **Needs revision** — specific issues that must be fixed before publication
- **Do not publish** — serious accuracy or liability concerns

### Post-Review (Editor)

- [ ] Incorporate all lawyer's edits
- [ ] Re-verify any changed facts
- [ ] Confirm all links still work
- [ ] Lawyer has given final approval
- [ ] Add publication date
- [ ] Schedule or publish post

## Disclaimer & Legal Notice Template

**Include this on every post:**

> ⚠️ **IMPORTANT LEGAL NOTICE**
> 
> This post is **informational only** and does **not** constitute legal advice. Immigration law is complex and varies by country, immigration status, and personal circumstances. **Always consult a qualified immigration lawyer before making decisions about your case.**
> 
> The information is accurate as of [publication date] but may change. Government procedures, timelines, and eligibility criteria are subject to change.
> 
> **Need legal help?**
> - Contact a local immigration lawyer
> - Reach out to a refugee support NGO (see links below)
> - Call a legal aid helpline in your country
> - Visit your government immigration office

## Liability Management

### What Creates Liability Risk
- Stating incorrect legal information
- Omitting material exceptions or conditions
- Implying advice ("You should...")
- Describing procedures as definitive when they vary
- Failing to explain complexity where it exists

### How We Minimize Risk
- Comprehensive legal review before publication
- Clear, prominent disclaimer on every post
- Source citations for all claims
- Neutral, factual tone
- Encouragement to seek professional advice
- Clear distinction between "what the law says" and "what this means"

### If There's an Error

If a published post contains an error:
1. Contact lawyer immediately
2. Publish a correction notice
3. Issue a revised post with update date
4. Note the correction in editorial log
5. Review process that caught error

## Finding an Immigration Lawyer for Review

**Qualifications:**
- Licensed immigration lawyer in relevant jurisdiction(s)
- Familiarity with asylum, family reunification, or visa law
- Experience with EU, French, Australian, or Canadian immigration (or combinations)
- Ability to review for accuracy and liability risk
- Reasonable turnaround time (2–3 days ideal)

**Where to Find:**
- Immigration law firms (search "[country] immigration lawyer")
- Law school immigration clinics (often provide free review)
- NGO legal advisory programs
- Professional association referral services
- Legal aid organizations

**Cost Considerations:**
- Some lawyers offer flat rates for content review
- Some NGOs provide free review for educational materials
- Budget per post: €50–200 depending on complexity
- Monthly retainer may be cost-effective if publishing 2–4x monthly

**Suggested Engagement:**
- Start with a small retainer agreement (e.g., 4 posts/month)
- Establish review turnaround SLA (e.g., 2 working days)
- Provide lawyer with templates upfront for consistency
- Build relationship for faster, more efficient reviews over time

## Timeline for Post Publication

**Ideal workflow:**
1. **Day 1:** Event occurs / law changes → editor begins draft
2. **Day 2:** Draft complete → lawyer begins review
3. **Day 3–4:** Lawyer returns feedback → editor revises
4. **Day 4–5:** Final approval → post published

**Note:** Urgent posts on breaking changes may be published faster with lawyer's expedited review.

---

## Post-Publication Monitoring

After publishing:
- [ ] Monitor for reader questions or concerns
- [ ] Flag any errors that emerge in reader feedback
- [ ] Review if subsequent law changes affect post accuracy
- [ ] Update or replace posts that become outdated
- [ ] Maintain version history of posts for reference
