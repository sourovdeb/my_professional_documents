## 💌 Outreach Templates

### Cold Email to Hiring Manager

```
Subject: [Your Name] - [Position] Interest at [Company]

Hi [Name],

I came across [Company Name]'s opening for [position] and was genuinely impressed by [specific company detail you researched].

My background in [skill 1] and [skill 2], combined with my experience [brief relevant achievement], align well with what you're looking for.

I'd love to chat briefly about how I can contribute to [company goal/project].

Best,
Sourov
[Your phone]
[Your LinkedIn]
```

### Cold Email to Potential Writing Partner

```
Subject: Collaboration Idea - [Project Name]

Hi [Name],

I've been following your work on [their project/writing], particularly your insights on [specific piece they wrote].

I'm working on [your project] and think our perspectives on [topic] could create something valuable together.

Would you be open to a quick coffee chat (virtual or otherwise) to explore this?

Best,
Sourov
[Your LinkedIn]
```

### Networking Email (No Ask Yet)

```
Subject: Quick Hello - [Mutual Connection]

Hi [Name],

[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out. I'm [brief bio - your story], currently focused on [current project].

I've admired your work on [specific thing], especially [specific detail].

Would be great to connect and hear about what you're working on.

Best,
Sourov
[Your LinkedIn]
```

## 📅 Outreach Schedule

**Weekly outreach goal**: Reach out to 3 new people

**Monday**: 1 hiring manager/recruiter

**Friday**: 1 mental health community member or mentor

**Time investment**: ~15 min per outreach

---

## 🔄 Follow-Up Strategy

**First email**: Initial outreach
**No response after 1 week**: Follow-up email
**No response after 2 weeks**: Try alternative contact method (LinkedIn message)
**No response after 3 weeks**: Mark as "no response" and move on

**Example follow-up**:
```
Subject: Re: [Original subject line]

Hi [Name],

Following up on my email from [date]. I know you're busy—no pressure at all.

If you do find time and are interested in [project/conversation], I'd love to connect.

Best,
Sourov
```

## 🤝 Building Real Relationships

**Don't just ask**: Build genuine interest first.

Better approach:
1. **Listen**: Read their blog, follow their work
2. **Genuinely appreciate**: Find something you actually admire
3. **Add value first**: Share something useful, make an introduction, offer help
4. **Then ask**: Only ask after you've given

## 🧠 Mental Health Community Building

**Why include this**: Mental health is part of your story, and connecting with others who get it is powerful.

### Online Communities
- **Reddit**: r/bipolar, r/depression, r/BipolarSOs
- **NAMI**: nami.org/get-involved (support groups)
- **DBSA**: dbsalliance.org (peer support)
- **7 Cups**: 7cups.com (peer emotional support)
- **Discord servers**: Search "mental health" on Discord

### How to Contribute
- Share your story (when you're ready)
- Help others (you've lived through struggles)
- Ask for advice (vulnerability is strength)
- Celebrate wins together

2. **Research & Add** (20 min)
   - Hiring managers in your field
   - Writers you admire
   - People from mental health communities
   - Add to appropriate CSV

3. **Send First Outreach** (Today)
   - Pick 1 person you want to reconnect with
   - Use "Networking Email" template
   - Send today

4. **Track** in Status column
   - Not contacted / Contacted / Following up / Active / Collaborated

## 📞 When to Reach Out

**Good times to contact**:
- Tuesday-Thursday (people are more responsive)
- 9am-11am or 2pm-4pm (working hours)
- Not Monday morning (busy) or Friday afternoon (checking out)

**Good reasons to reach out**:
- They posted about something you care about
- You have mutual connections
- You can offer something first
- You genuinely want to learn from them

**Bad reasons**:
- You need something immediately
- You haven't read their work
- It's a pure ask with no relationship foundation
- You're reaching out to 100 people with the same generic message
