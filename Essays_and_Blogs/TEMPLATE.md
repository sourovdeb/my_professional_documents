## The Opening

Start here with a personal observation, question, or small story relevant to your life. Make it specific. If you're writing about bipolar management, mention your own experience. If you're writing about automation, share why you care. The reader should feel your stake in this topic within the first paragraph.

**Word target**: 2-3 sentences, 30-50 words.

---

## The Main Idea

Now introduce your core insight. This is where you state what you've learned, tested, or discovered. Ground it in something real—a book, a tool, a conversation, therapy notes, or your own experimentation.

Keep it active: "I realized..." not "It was discovered that..."

**Word target**: 75-100 words.

---

## How This Applies to Your Life

Connect your idea back to bipolar management, mental health, job searching, or whatever context matters to you. Be honest. If you're struggling, say so. If this tool actually helped you, share it. Readers don't trust theory; they trust proof.

**Word target**: 100-120 words.

---

## Practical Actions

Give 2-3 things your reader can do today. Concrete steps. Links to tools if needed. Make it actionable.

**Example structure**:
1. First action: Why it works
2. Second action: How to start
3. Third action: What to track

**Word target**: 100-130 words.

---

## The Closing

End with a single takeaway. What will they remember? What will they try? Make it personal and hopeful.

**Word target**: 30-50 words.

---

## Your Reflection (For You, Not the Reader)

- **What did you learn writing this?**
- **Who needs to hear this?**
- **What official sources support your claims?**
- **Does this serve your story?**
