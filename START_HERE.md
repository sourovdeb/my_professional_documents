> One page. Everything links from here. Open this first, every day.

You asked for one thing made of many parts: get your story heard, write daily,
find work and good people, automate the boring parts, stay organised, and look
after your health while you do it. This folder turns that into a system you can

## ⚠️ Read this first (2 minutes) — it protects you

This GitHub repository is **PUBLIC**. Right now, anyone on the internet can read:

- Your **medical diagnosis** (recurrent depression, ADHD traits), treatment
  plans, and your **MDPH disability application**.
- Your **legal/employment dispute** documents (the Cambridge CELTA Stage 1 file).
- Your **life-story transcript** and personal CV with contact details.

In France, an employer who finds your diagnosis online can quietly discriminate,
and you would never be able to prove it. **Your health data is yours to disclose
on your terms — not the internet's to find.**

**Do this today (5 minutes):** Make the repo private.
GitHub → your repo → **Settings** → scroll to **Danger Zone** →
**Change repository visibility** → **Make private**.
Official guide: https://docs.github.com/en/repositories/managing-your-repositorys-settings-and-features/managing-repository-settings/setting-repository-visibility

Then keep a **separate, public repo** for only the things you *choose* to show
the world (your blog drafts, your tools, your portfolio). Private life in the
private repo; public face in the public repo. See `hub/REPO_ORGANISATION.md`.

**WordPress FTP / passwords:** never commit these to any repo, public or
private. I did not find them in plaintext, which is good. Keep them in a local
file the repo ignores — see `hub/guides/04_AUTOMATION.md` → "Secrets".

## The 20-minute daily loop (your minimum)

On a good day, do all five. On a hard day, do step 1 only — that still counts.

1. **Write 500 words** in `hub/content/blog/` (15 min). Quality over quantity:
   one clear idea, said like a human.
2. **Send 1 real message** to one person (a school, a writer, an old contact).
3. **Park 1 idea** in `hub/guides/05_IDEAS.md` so tomorrow is easier.
4. **Run the job check** (the automation does the searching; you just review).
5. **Close the laptop and do one kind thing for your body** (water, walk, rest).

That's the whole machine. The rest of this folder just makes each step easier.
