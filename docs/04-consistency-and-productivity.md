# Consistency & Productivity (That Respect Your Limits)

Consistency is not intensity. It is a low bar, cleared often, forgiven when
missed. The goal is a practice that survives your worst days — because those are
the days it has to survive to count.

## The Daily 500

One post, ~500 words, most days. Use `content/templates/daily-500.md`.

- **The bar is "open the file," not "write something good."** Momentum is
  assembled from small motions, not summoned by willpower.
- **300 rough words that exist beat 500 perfect words that don't.** Word count is
  negotiable. Showing up is not.
- **Missing a day is allowed. Quitting the practice is not.** A daily practice
  runs on forgiveness, not streaks. Don't let one gap become the excuse.

## Techniques that fit a variable-energy life

**1. Energy-tagged tasks.** In your ideas note (under `content/`), tag each idea
`[low]`, `[medium]`, or `[deep]`. Match the task to the day you actually have, not
the day you wish you had.

**2. The two-list day.** Each morning write two tiny lists: *must* (one thing)
and *bonus* (anything else). Clear the one. Everything past it is a gift.

**3. Timeboxing over to-do lists.** A 25-minute timer (Pomofocus) is kinder than
an open-ended task. When it rings, you may stop — guilt-free. Often you won't
want to. (See pacing in [docs/06](06-self-care-and-your-condition.md).)

**4. Capture, don't hold.** Ideas arrive uninvited. Dump them in your ideas note
the instant they appear so your mind isn't a storage unit.

**5. Separate the modes.** Drafting and editing use different parts of you. Don't
run them at once — it's why the page feels twice as heavy.

**6. Done beats perfect, then iterate.** Publish the imperfect thing. You can
always revise live. Perfectionism is fear in a respectable coat.

## A sustainable weekly rhythm (adapt freely)

| Day type | Aim |
|----------|-----|
| Good-energy day | Draft a `[deep]` piece; do distribution |
| Medium day | Daily 500 from a `[medium]` idea; reply to 3 writers |
| Low day | One `[low]` idea, edit yesterday's draft, or rest fully |
| Rest day | Rest. No guilt. Rest is part of the system, not a failure of it. |

## The one rule that holds it together

**Never trade your health for output.** A streak you sustain at 60% beats a sprint
that costs you a week in bed. The system is only good if it's still standing next
month.
