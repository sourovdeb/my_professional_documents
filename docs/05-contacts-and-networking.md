# Contacts & Networking (Without Groveling)

The useful network is small, real, and mutual. You are not collecting contacts;
you are finding a handful of people whose work you genuinely respect and being
useful to them. That's it. That's the whole game.

## Who to look for

- **Like-minded writers** — people writing on your themes (philosophy, health,
  craft, being heard). Future collaborators and friends.
- **Editors & publications** — who commission the kind of work you do.
- **Peers, not gatekeepers** — someone one step ahead of you is more reachable
  and more helpful than someone ten steps ahead.

Surface candidates with the assistant's connectors (search dev.to/Hashnode/
Mastodon, or ask it to find writers on your themes). The tools find; **you**
connect.

## How to actually connect (the only method that works)

1. **Read their work first. Really read it.** No connection survives a message
   that proves you didn't.
2. **Be useful before you ask.** Share their piece, leave a real comment, answer a
   question they posted. Give three times before you ask once.
3. **Be specific.** "I loved your essay" is noise. "The line about X named
   something I'd felt for years" is a relationship.
4. **Make the ask small and easy to refuse.** Easy-to-say-no-to asks get said yes
   to far more often.

## Outreach templates (adapt — never send verbatim)

**Cold note to a writer you admire**
> Hi {name} — your piece on {specific thing} stayed with me, especially {one
> genuine detail}. I write about {your themes} at {link}. No ask — just wanted you
> to know it landed. If you ever want a reader for a draft, I'd be glad to.

**Proposing a collaboration**
> Hi {name} — we both keep circling {shared theme}. I've been wondering about
> {concrete small project: a joint essay, a shared prompt, swapping drafts}.
> Low-pressure and no deadline. Would that be fun for you?

**Following up on a job (after applying)**
> Hi {name} — I applied for {role} on {date} and wanted to add that {one specific,
> relevant thing}. I'm genuinely excited about {specific thing about them}. Happy
> to share more whenever useful.

## Keep a tiny CRM

Don't trust memory. Keep a simple, private, gitignored file (`contacts.local.md`):

```
## {Name} — {what they do} — {link}
- 2026-05-31: shared their essay, left a comment
- next: reply if they respond; suggest draft-swap in ~2 weeks
```

Small, warm, consistent follow-up beats any growth hack. Ten real relationships
will do more for your career and your spirit than ten thousand followers.
