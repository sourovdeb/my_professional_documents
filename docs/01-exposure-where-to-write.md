# Exposure: Where to Write, and How People Find You

Exposure is not luck. It is the same small piece, placed where the right people
already gather, repeated until they recognize your name. You do not need every
platform. You need **one home base you own** and **one or two places you visit
to be seen.**

## The hub-and-spoke model

- **Hub (you own it):** your WordPress site. Nobody can take it, throttle it, or
  change the rules on you. Everything you write lives here first.
- **Spokes (you borrow the audience):** platforms where readers already are. You
  republish or adapt your hub posts here, always linking back home.

Write once on the hub. Adapt to one or two spokes. Repeat.

## Where to publish (free tiers, ranked by fit for personal/essay writing)

| Platform | Why it fits you | Link |
|----------|-----------------|------|
| **WordPress.org** (self-hosted) | Your hub. Full control, your FTP. | https://wordpress.org |
| **Substack** | Best free tool for building an email list of true fans. | https://substack.com |
| **Medium** | Built-in readers for essays + personal/philosophy topics. | https://medium.com |
| **Ghost** | Open-source, beautiful, owns-your-list (paid hosting or self-host). | https://ghost.org |
| **dev.to** | If any writing is technical — engaged, kind community. | https://dev.to |
| **Hashnode** | Technical writing, free custom domain. | https://hashnode.com |
| **Mastodon** | Open, no algorithm games; good for daily small thoughts. | https://joinmastodon.org |

**Owned email beats every algorithm.** A list of 200 people who chose you is
worth more than 20,000 followers a platform can hide tomorrow. Start the list now,
even at zero subscribers.

## What gets seen

People share writing that does one of these:

1. **Names a feeling they couldn't.** Your essays on starting, on energy, on being
   heard — these are this. Lean in.
2. **Helps them do something.** "Tools I use to write without friction."
3. **Tells a true story.** Your story and philosophy *are* the differentiator.
   Nobody else has them.

Pick one per piece. Don't try to do all three at once.

## The honest distribution checklist (per post)

- [ ] Publish on the hub (WordPress) first.
- [ ] Adapt the opening for one spoke and cross-post with a link home.
- [ ] Post one short, genuine thread/note pointing to it (Mastodon/LinkedIn).
- [ ] Reply thoughtfully to 3 other people's posts that day. Connection > broadcast.
- [ ] Capture the idea's offshoots in your ideas note under `content/`.

That's it. Fifteen minutes of distribution on top of the writing. No more — the
writing is the work; distribution is the echo.

## What *not* to do

- Don't post the same thing everywhere at once on day one. Stagger it.
- Don't buy followers or engagement. It poisons the signal you need.
- Don't chase virality. Chase the **right ten readers.** They compound.
