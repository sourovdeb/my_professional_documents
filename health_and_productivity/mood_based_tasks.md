# Mood-Based Task Selection

**When you can't decide what to do: use your mood to choose the task.**

This prevents overcommitting when depleted and maximizes output when energized.

---

## Energy 9-10: Hypomanic/Manic

**Characteristics:** Lots of ideas, racing thoughts, less need for sleep, everything feels important, impulsive

**Risk:** Burn out, regrettable emails/commits, overcommit, sleep deprivation

**Tasks (YES):**
- Write multiple essays (batch them)
- Create new automation scripts
- Reach out to collaborators/partners
- Record videos/podcasts
- Research deep dives
- Plan quarterly roadmap
- Organize archives

**Caution:** 
- Force yourself to sleep 8 hours (non-negotiable)
- Review all emails/commits 24h before sending
- Don't make big decisions
- Take medication as prescribed
- Check in with your psychiatrist/therapist

**Daily minimum:** Write in email/message: "I'm feeling manic today. I'll review all outreach before sending." This creates accountability.

---

## Energy 7-8: Normal High (Optimal)

**Characteristics:** Focused, creative, stable, can work 3-4 hours without break, ideas are clear

**Tasks (YES):**
- Write 1-2 essays
- Email outreach to 5-10 contacts
- Research + document findings
- Code new features
- Record explanatory videos
- Update job tracking spreadsheet
- Edit and format existing drafts

**This is your "wins" time.** Do important work now. It compounds.

**Daily goal:** 1 essay + 1 hour of job search/outreach = your baseline good day

---

## Energy 5-6: Normal (Baseline)

**Characteristics:** Stable, slightly tired after 2 hours, can do most things but not all

**Tasks (YES):**
- Edit existing essays
- Organize files and folders
- Update tracking spreadsheets
- Research (no writing yet)
- Schedule posts for publication
- Reply to emails (thoughtful, not bulk)
- Format documents for WordPress
- Read sources and take notes

**Tasks (NO):**
- Writing from scratch (too effortful)
- Big decisions
- New projects

**Daily goal:** Keep the system running. Don't break streak. Do one small creative task.

---

## Energy 3-4: Tired (Depleted)

**Characteristics:** Brain fog, can't focus >30 min, words are hard, but not depressed

**Tasks (YES):**
- Use essay templates to write (50% pre-written)
- Organize files (passive task)
- Review job postings (read, don't apply)
- Update energy tracker
- Take notes on sources (no synthesis)
- Respond to urgent emails only
- Publish pre-written posts
- Listen to audiobooks/podcasts on your topics

**Tasks (NO):**
- Anything new
- Anything that requires decision-making
- Group calls (if you can avoid)

**Your job now:** Protect yourself. Rest is productive.

**Daily goal:** One small task. Protect your streak by just logging that you rested.

---

## Energy 1-2: Depressive/Very Low

**Characteristics:** Everything is hard, negative self-talk, isolation urge, time moves weird, sleep disrupted

**Tasks (YES):**
- Push pre-written essays to WordPress (zero creativity)
- Read your own success (scroll back through essays you wrote)
- Bath, sleep, medication check
- Text one friend (low-pressure)
- Drink water, eat something
- Log energy level (you're tracking, that counts)
- Listen to music or nature sounds
- Watch something comforting

**Tasks (NO):**
- Nothing. You don't have tasks right now.
- NO guilt about missing essays
- NO "I should be writing"

**This is a medical event, not laziness.**

**Your job now:** Survive the day with medication, sleep, minimal pressure.

**Daily goal:** Take medication. Eat something. Sleep. That's it. Tell someone "I'm in an episode" if you can.

## By Context: Specific Situations

### If You Get Rejected (Email, Job, Collaboration)

**Energy drops immediately.** This is normal for bipolar people. Your amygdala is overactive.

**Do:**
1. Don't respond. At all. 24h rule absolute.
2. Move to Energy 3-4 tasks (protect yourself)
3. Call someone or write in your journal
4. Remember: rejection is data, not a reflection of you
5. Wait 48-72h before deciding if it's "the end"

**Avoid:** Looking for "better" opportunities the same day. You'll make impulsive decisions.

---

### If You Didn't Sleep Well

**Assume energy is -1 from normal.** Sleep disruption flips bipolar switches.

**Do:**
- Take your medication (even if you don't feel like it)
- Stick to 24h delay rule on all communications
- Shift down one energy tier in task selection
- Go to bed at 8 PM tonight (non-negotiable)

**Avoid:** Pushing through. Sleep debt compounds fast in bipolar disorder.

---

### If Your Medication Changed

**Energy is unpredictable for 2-3 weeks.** Don't commit to schedules.

**Do:**
- Track energy obsessively (every day)
- Stick to 3-4 tasks (routine, not ambition)
- Tell your contacts: "I'm adjusting meds, slower response time"
- Keep check-ins with psychiatrist

**Avoid:** Making career decisions, big launches, or new commitments.

---

### If You're in a Good Week (4+ days energy 7+)

**This is harvest time.** Batch your work:

**Day 1-2 (Energy 8-9):** Write 3-4 essays, outline automation scripts  
**Day 3 (Energy 7-8):** Do outreach, schedule social posts  
**Day 4 (Energy 6-7):** Edit, format, publish  
**Day 5 (Energy drops):** Rest, recover, celebrate

This rhythm prevents burnout and gives you buffer for low weeks.

## Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Typical Energy | Task Type | Time |
|-----|---|---|---|
| **Monday** | 6-7 | Plan week, catch up emails | 30 min |
| **Tuesday** | 7-8 | Write, research, outreach | 2-3 hrs |
| **Wednesday** | 6-7 | Edit, format, organize | 1-2 hrs |
| **Thursday** | 5-6 | Publish, schedule, respond | 1 hr |
| **Friday** | 5-6 | Lighter work, maybe journal | 30 min |
| **Saturday** | Varies | Flexible | Rest or catch-up |
| **Sunday** | 6-7 | Review, plan next week | 45 min |

**This is a template.** Your rhythm will be different. Track what actually happens in your energy_tracker.md.

---

## Red Flags: When to Skip Everything

Contact your psychiatrist or therapist **immediately** if:

- [ ] Suicidal thoughts or self-harm urges
- [ ] 3+ days without sleep (manic) or sleeping 12+ hours (depressed)
- [ ] Complete isolation (no contact with anyone)
- [ ] Medication not working / side effects unbearable
- [ ] You've made a major decision in manic state (spent money, quit job, ended relationship)
- [ ] Substance use increases (alcohol, drugs)

**When these happen:** Stop all work. Call your doctor. Tell someone. The essays and jobs can wait.

