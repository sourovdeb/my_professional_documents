# 05 — Consistency & Self-Care

The whole engine is worthless if it burns you out. This file makes the writing *durable*.
It sits on top of your existing **Daily Living Strategy** and **Productivity, Health &
Income Plan** — read those for the full floor; this is the writing-specific layer.

> Nothing here is medical advice. Dosing, diagnosis and treatment stay with Dr. Pauvert
> and your psychiatric review. This is daily-living scaffolding built on official,
> evidence-based sources (NIMH, etc.) and on your own documented plan.

## Productivity technique: route the task to the state

You already named the principle — *the system carries the day the person can't.* Applied
to writing:

| Today I am… | Write this (from file 01's five types) | Tool |
|---|---|---|
| **Up / sharp / fast** | A **Take** or a **System** essay; batch-draft 2–3 posts to bank for crash days | Obsidian, keyboard |
| **Flat / low** | A **Story** or **Field note** — talk it out loud, clean later | Whisper / voice notes |
| **Scattered / can't start** | Capture 5 ideas to `ideas.md`; ship a banked draft | timer, banked drafts |
| **Crash day** | Four anchors only. Posting a banked draft = bonus, not required | — |

**Banking is your insurance.** On good days, write ahead. A 5-post buffer means your
public streak never depends on your worst day. This is the single most important habit
for consistency with a cycling mood.

## The technique stack (proven, pick what fits)
- **Time-blocking + a 25-min timer** (Pomodoro). One block = one draft. Interest-based attention (Dodson) means a timer + a topic you care about beats willpower.
- **Implementation intention:** "After my morning meds + coffee, I write 500 words." Anchor the habit to an existing anchor.
- **Two-minute start:** open the file and write one ugly sentence. Starting is the whole battle; momentum does the rest.
- **Don't break the chain:** the `GROWTH/log.md` streak is the only KPI. A visible streak is dopamine that works *for* you.
- **Body double / write with someone:** a focusmate-style co-working call or a friend on a call makes starting easier on an ADHD brain.

## Staying consistent without self-harm
- **Define "done" tiny.** Done = 500 words shipped, not "a great post." Perfectionism is the enemy of a streak.
- **A miss is one day, not a failure.** Never miss *twice*. Skip a day, return next day — the streak survives a gap, not a spiral.
- **Protect the anchor times.** Same wake, meds, one meal, ship-time. Routine regulates mood independent of energy (social rhythm therapy).
- **Separate making from editing.** Draft with the critic off; edit later, briefly. Two modes, never at once.

## Taking care of yourself (the floor under the work)
From NIMH/evidence-based guidance and your own plan:
- **Sleep is the lever.** Fixed wake time 7 days/week; screens off ~1h before bed; aim 7–9h. Sleep loss is an early manic trigger — guard it harder than any deadline.
- **Move daily, gently.** A walk in daylight counts. Exercise eases depression/anxiety and improves sleep.
- **One real meal, fixed time.** A rhythm cue, not just food.
- **Mood/life chart.** Track mood, sleep, energy daily (NIMH life-chart method). It catches a swing *before* it catches you — and it's free content for your "Field note" posts.
- **Keep your team close.** Monthly psychiatric review, GP, and at least one human who knows your early-warning signs.

## What to learn about your condition (study in public)
Turn learning into both stability *and* content. Use **official, reputable sources only**:
- **NIMH** — Bipolar Disorder & ADHD plain-language guides + the life-chart method.
- **NHS** (nhs.uk) — practical living-with guides.
- **France:** HAS (Haute Autorité de Santé) recommendations; Santé publique France; patient associations (e.g. Argos 2001 for bipolar). For rights/work: MDPH, RQTH, Cap Emploi.
- **Evidence-based therapies to know:** CBT, family-focused therapy, **IPSRT** (interpersonal & social rhythm therapy — directly behind your "fixed wake/anchor" approach), behavioural activation for low days.

**Learning loop:** read one official thing → apply one change → write one "Field note"
post about it. Stability and exposure from the same 20 minutes.

> Always verify against official sources and your clinical team. If you ever feel a swing
> coming — especially less sleep + more energy + more ideas — that is a signal to *slow
> the engine*, not feed it. The work will keep. You come first.
