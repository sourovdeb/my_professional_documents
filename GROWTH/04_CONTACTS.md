# 04 — Contacts

Exposure makes the introduction; outreach closes it. The writing brings people to you —
this file is how you reach the ones who won't find you on their own. You already have a
`MASTER_CONTACT_DIRECTORY.md` and `CONTACTS_AND_EMAILS_FOUR_CHANNELS.md` in your archive —
this builds the *practice* on top of them.

## The system (works on a low-energy brain)

1. **One list, one place.** A simple table (Obsidian/CSV): `Name | Org | Role | Channel | Last touch | Next step`. Feeds the email extension in file 03.
2. **The warm-up before the ask.** Follow → read → leave one substantive comment → *then* message. By the time you reach out, you're not a stranger.
3. **Reach out to 3 people a week.** Not 30. Three real, personal messages beat a blast. Recycle a template, change two specific lines per person.
4. **Always make it easy to say yes.** End with one clear, small ask: a reply, a call, a referral, or "here's my CV, I'm open to English-training and writing work."
5. **Follow up once, politely, after ~7 days.** Most replies come from the follow-up. Then let it go.

## The one-message template (adapt, never paste cold)

> Hi [Name] — I read your [specific thing] and [one genuine, specific sentence].
> I'm a CELTA-certified English trainer (IELTS/TOEIC/Business English) and I write daily
> about [teaching / building a working life with ADHD & bipolar]. [One line on why I'm
> reaching out to *them* specifically.] Would you be open to [small ask]? Either way,
> thanks for [their work]. — Sourov

## Turn every essay into a contact funnel
Every post ends with the same quiet line: *"I'm open to English-training and writing work
— CV and contact here."* Link to your `Profile_Documents/` CV and a contact form. Exposure
without a door is wasted; give every reader a door.

## Keeping relationships warm (cheap, high-return)
- When someone helps, **say thanks specifically** and tell them the outcome later. People remember closure.
- **Resurface quarterly:** a short "here's what I've been writing / I thought of you when…" note. No ask. Keeps you top-of-mind for when a role opens.
- **Give before you take:** share their work, answer their questions, refer people to them. A reputation for usefulness is the best automation there is.

## Disclosure (your call, your timing)
You have a documented `SKILL_neurodiversity-disclosure-documentation.md`. Disclosing
bipolar/ADHD is **always your choice** and can come *after* an offer, to access
accommodations (in France: via MDPH/RQTH, Cap Emploi). Some employers actively seek
neurodiverse talent — your honest writing pre-qualifies those allies for you. Lead with
your work; disclose on your terms.
