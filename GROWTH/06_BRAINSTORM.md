# 06 — Brainstorm: what we can actually build

A menu, not a to-do list. Each idea names **what** it is and **why** it works *for you
specifically* — given your assets: CELTA/IELTS/TOEIC/Business English, fluent French +
English, life in La Réunion, a documented lived experience of bipolar I + ADHD, a public
GitHub body of work, and a real appetite for automation. Pick what pulls you; ignore the
rest. Starred (★) = highest leverage for lowest effort.

---

## A. Practical — income and proof, soon

1. **★ "Build-in-public" teaching repo → clients.** Keep publishing your CELTA frameworks and lesson ideas openly. *Why:* every post is a searchable audition; schools and private students hire the teacher they can already see teaching. Your repo *is* your CV.
2. **★ Productised tutoring.** Three fixed packages — "IELTS 6→7 in 8 weeks," "Business English for French professionals," "TOEIC sprint." *Why:* fixed scope beats hourly for an ADHD brain (no negotiation, no admin spiral) and converts readers into paying students directly from your posts.
3. **Paid newsletter tier later.** Free essays build the audience; a paid tier offers lesson packs, templates, office hours. *Why:* income that doesn't trade hours for money on low-energy days.
4. **Bilingual edge.** Sell to French speakers who need English *and* to English speakers who need French/relocation help. *Why:* La Réunion + bilingualism is a niche almost no Anglophone competitor can serve.
5. **★ Templatise your own systems as products.** The Daily Living Strategy, the email-draft extension, the job-search skills — package them as a Notion/Obsidian kit or a small paid download. *Why:* you already built them for yourself; selling the byproduct is free money.
6. **Remote-first job funnel.** Use the automation in `03` to surface remote online-teaching and ed-tech roles weekly. *Why:* remote work fits a body that has uneven days far better than a fixed classroom timetable.

## B. Creative — story and reputation

7. **★ Essay series: "The system carries the day."** Your philosophy, one tactic per post, eventually a short e-book. *Why:* it's genuinely original productivity thinking, not recycled advice — that's what gets shared and remembered.
8. **"Teaching English with a brain that won't sit still."** A column on ADHD/bipolar *as a teacher*, not just a patient. *Why:* almost no one writes this from the front of the classroom; you'd own the niche.
9. **Memoir in 500-word pieces.** Quietly serialise your life story (already written in `Story_of_Sourov`) post by post. *Why:* a memoir feels impossible; 500 words a day is a memoir by accident inside a year.
10. **Field notes from La Réunion.** The island, the French system, teaching at the edge of the map. *Why:* place is a hook; it makes your writing unmistakably yours and travels well internationally.
11. **A small podcast or voice series.** On flat days, *talk* a post with Whisper instead of typing. *Why:* turns your worst-format day into a second content channel, and voice carries warmth text can't.

## C. Unorthodox — bets most people won't make

12. **★ Disclosure as a moat, not a risk.** Build your whole public brand on radical honesty about bipolar/ADHD + high-functioning work. *Why:* vulnerability that's *competent* is magnetic and uncopyable — competitors can fake expertise, not your story. It also pre-filters for employers and clients who actually want you.
13. **Open-source your operating system for living.** Publish the anonymised Daily Living Strategy as a public template others with cycling moods can fork. *Why:* it positions you as a thinker/leader in neurodivergent productivity, not just a job-seeker — and leaders get offers, not applications.
14. **★ Turn the repo into the product.** Most people hide their work-in-progress; you've shown the whole machine. Lean in: make `my_professional_documents` a *showcase* of "here's how one neurodivergent professional runs his entire career in public." *Why:* it's a living portfolio, a credibility engine, and a magnet for like-minded collaborators — all at once.
15. **Teach the tools you use to teach.** Workshops for other teachers / neurodivergent freelancers: "automate your admin so you can just teach." *Why:* sells your *meta*-skill (systems) to your *own* community — higher rate than tutoring, infinite supply of clients.
16. **AI-assisted "twin" for your worst days.** Use Ollama + your own writing to draft in your voice when you can't start. *Why:* a personalised model trained on your published essays keeps the streak alive on crash days — the ultimate version of "the system carries the day."
17. **Collaboration callouts.** End essays with an open invitation: "I'm looking for one co-writer on X." *Why:* you stop hunting partners and let them self-select toward you — far higher quality than cold outreach.
18. **Grant / disability-innovation angle.** France and the EU fund neurodiversity-at-work and ed-tech projects. Position the systems you're already building as a fundable project. *Why:* non-dilutive money for work you'd do anyway; and a grant is also a credential.

---

## How to choose (so this doesn't become another overwhelm)

- **This quarter, pick ONE from A, ONE from B, ONE from C.** No more.
- Suggested starter trio: **#1 (build-in-public teaching)** + **#7 (the system series)** + **#14 (repo as showcase)**. They reinforce each other and need nothing you don't already have.
- Everything else goes in `GROWTH/ideas.md` and waits. A menu you can't finish is a feature, not a failure — it means you'll never run out of next moves.

> The throughline: you already *did* the hard part — you built the systems, wrote the
> story, certified the skill. Almost every idea here is just **pointing existing assets
> at the public.** Low new effort, high compounding return. That's the whole strategy.
