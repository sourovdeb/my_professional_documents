# 01 — Write & Exposure

How 500 words a day becomes an audience, a reputation, and a way in.

## Where to publish

Pick **one home base** (you own the readers) + **one or two distribution channels**
(you borrow the reach). Don't spread thin — quality over quantity.

### Home base — pick ONE

| Platform | Why | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Ghost** (recommended) | Open-source, you own your list and data, 0% revenue cut, clean writing UI, built-in newsletter. Self-host free, or Ghost(Pro) managed. | Free self-hosted / paid managed |
| **Substack** | Fastest start, built-in discovery and recommendations, network of readers. Takes 10% if you ever charge. | Free |
| **Beehiiv** | Strong growth tools, full audience ownership, no revenue share. | Free tier |
| **WordPress** | Maximum control, open-source, huge ecosystem. More setup. | Free self-hosted |
| **Buttondown** | Minimal, writer-first, privacy-friendly newsletter. | Free tier |

**My call:** Start on **Substack** for the built-in discovery (you need readers, not a
server to maintain). The moment you have ~500 subscribers, mirror to **Ghost** so you
own everything. Migration is easy; don't wait for perfect.

### Distribution — borrow reach
- **LinkedIn** — your professional graph + recruiters live here. Post the same essay (or a hook + link). Highest job/contact payoff for you.
- **Medium** — discovery engine; republish with canonical link back to your home base.
- **X / Bluesky / Mastodon** — one thread per essay, optional.

You already publish to Substack, Medium and LinkedIn (see `Communications/`). Good —
this isn't new, it's *consistent now*.

## What to write — you already have the material

Your repo is a goldmine. You're not inventing content; you're *cutting* it.

1. **Your story** — `Story_of_Sourov`, `Biography_and_Medical/TRANSCRIPT...LIFE_STORY.md`, `BIOPIC_SOUROV_DEB.md`. Bipolar I + ADHD + building a life and a teaching career in La Réunion. This is rare, honest, and magnetic.
2. **Your philosophy** — the "system carries the day the person can't" idea from your Daily Living Strategy is a *genuinely original* productivity philosophy. Write that series.
3. **Your craft** — CELTA / IELTS / TOEIC / Business English teaching. Practical, searchable, recruiter-relevant (`CELTA_Teaching_Materials/`).
4. **Your fight** — navigating the French system, MDPH/disability registration, the Cambridge case. Useful to others in the same maze (anonymise where needed).
5. **What you learn** — turn each thing you research about your condition or your tools into a short post. Learning in public = exposure + retention.

### Five repeatable column types (so you never face a blank page)
- **Story** — one scene from your life, one lesson.
- **System** — one tactic that keeps your day standing up.
- **Teach** — one concrete English-teaching idea readers can use today.
- **Field note** — one thing you learned this week (condition, tool, the system).
- **Take** — one honest opinion on neurodiversity, work, or learning.

Rotate them. Monday Story, Tuesday System, etc. Routing the *type* to your *mood* (see
file 05) means you can always write *something*.

## The 500-word method (≈25–40 min)

1. **Capture** (anytime): keep a running `ideas.md`. One line each. Never write from blank.
2. **Draft** (one timer block): open yesterday's idea, write 500 words, don't edit.
3. **Cut** (10 min): delete 15%. Active voice. One idea per post. Strong first line, strong last line.
4. **Ship**: publish to home base, cross-post the hook to LinkedIn. Log it.

Rules that compound:
- **Active voice, human, not monotone.** Vary sentence length. Write like you talk.
- **One idea per piece.** 500 words can't hold two.
- **First line is a promise; last line is a turn.** People decide in the first sentence.
- **Ship at the same time every day.** Consistency beats brilliance for exposure.

## How exposure actually compounds

- **Volume × consistency** — 250 posts/year is a body of work no one can ignore. Search and recommendation engines reward consistency.
- **Searchable titles** — write titles people type into Google ("How I teach IELTS writing with ADHD"). Evergreen traffic finds you while you sleep.
- **A repeatable angle** — be *the* person who writes honestly about building a teaching career with bipolar disorder. Specific beats broad.
- **Always end with one CTA** — subscribe, reply, or "open to English-training and writing work — here's my CV." That converts readers into jobs and contacts.
