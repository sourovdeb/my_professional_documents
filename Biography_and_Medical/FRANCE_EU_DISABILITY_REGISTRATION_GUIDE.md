### STEP 1: RQTH (Reconnaissance de la Qualité de Travailleur Handicapé)

**What it is:**
Official status recognising you as a disabled worker. Not a medical label — an administrative status.

**What it gives you:**
- Priority for employment support (AGEFIPH, FIPHFP)
- Employers have legal obligation to employ 6% disabled workers (OETH)
- Access to adapted training programs
- Protection against discrimination in hiring/firing
- Specific job placement assistance (Cap Emploi)
- Tax advantages for employers who hire you

**Your eligibility:**
✅ Yes — Bipolar Type I alone qualifies. ADHD, C-PTSD, depression all further support your claim.

**How to apply:**
1. Download CERFA form 15692 (dossier MDPH) from service-public.fr
2. Complete with your doctor (Dr. Pauvert or Dr. Padovani)
3. Attach:
   - Dr. Padovani's psychiatric letter (19 May 2026) — you have this
   - Medical certificate from Dr. Pauvert
   - Proof of identity (CNI or passport)
   - Proof of residence (last 3 months)
4. Submit to MDPH de La Réunion

**Timeline:** 4 months average (can be faster with complete dossier)
**Duration once granted:** 1–10 years (renewable)
**Cost:** Free

---

### STEP 2: AAH (Allocation aux Adultes Handicapés)

**What it is:**
Monthly financial allowance for disabled adults who cannot work at full capacity.

**Amount (2026):** ~971 € per month

**Your eligibility:**
✅ Likely eligible — you are on sick leave since late 2024, have confirmed Bipolar I + ADHD + depression, cannot currently sustain full-time employment.

**Conditions:**
- Disability rate ≥ 80% (assessed by MDPH) → AAH without employment conditions
- OR disability rate 50–79% with substantial restriction on access to employment
- Not exceed income thresholds

**How to apply:**
- Same CERFA dossier as RQTH (submitted to MDPH)
- MDPH assesses disability rate
- Pôle Emploi / CAF administers payments once MDPH grants

**Duration:** Granted for 1–5 years, then renewable
**Cost:** Free

---

### STEP 3: PCH (Prestation de Compensation du Handicap)

**What it is:**
Allowance to compensate for extra costs caused by disability (transport, equipment, support person).

**Your potential uses:**
- Cost of psychiatric medications (Concerta, etc.)
- Transport to medical appointments
- Adaptive equipment (noise-cancelling headphones, organisational tools)
- Part-time support worker

**Eligibility:**
- Under 60
- Disability impairs ability to perform daily activities
- ✅ Yes — Bipolar, ADHD, depression all qualify

**Amount:** Variable (assessed case by case, up to 1700 €/month)
**How:** MDPH application (same dossier)

## PART 4: HEALTHCARE RIGHTS

### ALD (Affection Longue Durée)
**What it is:**
Status for people with serious chronic conditions — you pay NOTHING for healthcare related to the ALD condition.

**Your conditions that likely qualify:**
- Bipolar Disorder Type I (ALD 23 — troubles mentaux)
- Severe depression (may qualify as part of ALD 23)

**How to apply:**
- Ask Dr. Pauvert (GP) to file an ALD request
- He sends to Sécurité Sociale (CPAM La Réunion)
- CPAM reviews and approves
- From then on: ZERO cost for all care related to bipolar/mental health

**What it covers:**
- All psychiatric appointments (Dr. Padovani)
- All related medications (Effexor, Concerta, etc.)
- All related tests (sleep study, EEG, MRI)
- Hospitalisation if needed

**Cost:** Free to apply
**Timeline:** Usually 1–2 months
**Priority:** HIGH — apply immediately. Your medications are expensive without ALD.

## PART 5: EU DISABILITY FRAMEWORK

### European Disability Card (Carte Européenne d'Invalidité)
**Status:** Pilot programme in France (as of 2025)
**What it gives:** Recognised in 8 EU countries; discounts on transport, culture, services
**How:** Issued via MDPH once you have disability recognition

### EU Disability Strategy 2021–2030
France is bound by this strategy. Key rights:
- Right to independent living and participation
- Right to quality employment
- Right to education and training
- Right to accessibility (public services, transport, information)
- Right to equality and non-discrimination

**For your CELTA case:**
- If the centre was delivering training funded by French public funds (CPF/OPCO)
- And they failed their duty under RQTH/disability accommodation law
- This may additionally constitute a breach of EU framework
- Relevant for your Ofqual complaint + potential legal action in France

## PART 6: CELTA COMPLAINT & DISABILITY LAW INTERSECTION

### French Law Applicable:
- **Loi n° 2005-102** (11 February 2005) — Définition du handicap, obligation d'aménagement raisonnable
- **Loi n° 2018-771** (5 September 2018) — Extends disability rights in vocational training
- **Code du Travail L5213** — RQTH employment protections
- **Règlement Qualiopi** — Quality certification including disability accommodation obligations
- **RGPD / CNIL** — Protection of your health data (misused if shared without consent)

### What This Means for Your Case:
The ELT Hub holds Qualiopi certification. Under Qualiopi:
- Criterion 3: The organisation must adapt to needs of learners
- Including neurodivergent learners with disclosed conditions
- Failure to accommodate = violation of Qualiopi criteria

**Potential action:** Report to Organisme Certificateur Qualiopi (probably Bureau Veritas or AFNOR depending on their certifier)
→ They can withdraw or suspend Qualiopi certification
