# Health-First Productivity Framework
## For Bipolar II + Depression Management

**Core Philosophy:** Sustainable creation > Consistent burnout

You have bipolar II disorder with depression. Your productivity system must work WITH your brain, not against it.

---

## Understanding Your Energy Patterns

### High Energy Phases (Hypomania-Adjacent)
**Duration:** 1-3 weeks (typically)  
**Energy Level:** 8-10/10  
**Mood:** Elevated, optimistic, motivated  
**Productivity:** HIGH

**What you CAN do:**
- Deep research & writing (essays, long-form content)
- Strategic planning (quarterly goals, new projects)
- Networking (calls, emails, outreach)
- Learning new tools & frameworks
- Complex problem-solving

**What to AVOID:**
- Ovcommitting (say yes to too many projects)
- Perfectionism (aim for good, not perfect)
- Sleep disruption (maintain schedule anyway)
- Substance use (don't self-medicate)

**DO THIS:** Batch-write 3-4 essays. Record video tutorials. Create templates for low-energy phases.

---

### Baseline Energy (Euthymia)
**Duration:** Most of the year (goal)  
**Energy Level:** 5-7/10  
**Mood:** Stable, neutral, functional  
**Productivity:** STEADY

**What you CAN do:**
- Edit & polish existing work
- Respond to messages & comments
- Learning (reading, courses)
- Administration (filing, organizing)
- Gentle networking
- Run automation scripts

**What to AVOID:**
- New major projects
- Challenging problem-solving
- High-stakes meetings
- Comparing yourself to others

**DO THIS:** Publish pre-written essays. Update tracking sheets. Maintain automation scripts.

---

### Low Energy Phases (Depression)
**Duration:** 3-14 days typically  
**Energy Level:** 1-4/10  
**Mood:** Flat, hopeless, exhausted  
**Productivity:** MINIMAL (accept this)

**What you CAN do:**
- Small admin tasks (5-15 min max)
- Run pre-written automation scripts
- Read others' content
- Rest (genuinely, without guilt)
- Light journaling
- Call therapist if needed

**What to AVOID:**
- Forcing productivity
- Major decisions
- Reaching out to new contacts
- Comparing to others
- Perfectionism

**DO THIS:** Let automation handle it. Publish pre-written essays. Rest guilt-free. Check health log.

## Weekly Template Framework

### Baseline Week (Good Energy)

```
MONDAY (8 AM):
  - Automation runs (job hunter, writer discovery)
  - Review results in Google Sheet
  - Light admin (20 min)

TUESDAY (Morning):
  - Research new essay topic (1-2 hours)
  - Take notes in Google Sheet
  - Commit sources to git

WEDNESDAY:
  - Write first draft (2-3 hours, split if needed)
  - Save to BLOG/DRAFTS/YYYY-MM/
  - Commit with message "Draft: [topic]"

THURSDAY (8 AM):
  - Automation runs
  - Edit yesterday's draft (light pass, 45 min)
  - Reply to messages/comments
  - Polish for publishing

FRIDAY:
  - Final polish (30 min)
  - Copy to BLOG/PUBLISHED/
  - Create WordPress branch
  - Update publishing_tracker.csv
  - Schedule post

WEEKEND:
  - REST. Genuinely.
  - Optional: light reading, inspiration hunting
  - No pressure to create
```

### High Energy Week (Hypomania)

Opportunity! Use this time to create bank of content.

```
MONDAY-FRIDAY:
  - Write 2-3 essays (aim for depth, not speed)
  - Record 1-2 video tutorials
  - Create 5-10 social media posts (batch)
  - Reach out to 3-5 potential collaborators
  - Document new tools/processes
  - Plan Q3 content themes

STORE EVERYTHING:
  - Essays → BLOG/DRAFTS/ (dated)
  - Videos → Google Drive (link in README)
  - Posts → social_media/scheduled/ (batch scheduler)
  - Processes → tools/documentation/
  - Contacts → TRACKING/contacts_discovered.csv
```

**Important:** You're not running a marathon. You're filling the pantry for winter. Rest afterward.

### Low Energy Week (Depression)

**Goal:** Maintain, don't create. Use your banks.

```
MONDAY-FRIDAY:
  - Run automation (5 min each)
  - Publish 1 pre-written essay (Friday)
  - Reply to urgent messages only
  - Log mood in health_log.csv
  - Do 1 small admin task daily
  - Be kind to yourself

CONTACT THERAPIST if:
  - Suicidal thoughts
  - Unable to get out of bed
  - Hopelessness lasting >3 days
  - Sleep disruption >2 nights

THIS IS NORMAL. Not lazy. Not failure.
```

## Content Banking System

### What is Content Banking?

Write when energy is HIGH, publish when energy is NORMAL or LOW.

**Example:**
- June 1-10 (high energy) → Write 4 essays
- June 11-20 (baseline) → Publish 1/week + edit others
- June 21-30 (depression) → Publish pre-written, rest
- July 1-10 (baseline) → Manage + plan next cycle

### How to Bank Content

**Create folder structure:**
```
BLOG/DRAFTS/
├── Q2-2026/  (quarterly banks)
│   ├── essay-1.md (draft)
│   ├── essay-2.md (ready to publish)
│   └── essay-3.md (polished)
└── publishing_tracker.csv (update status)
```

**Status levels:**
1. **DRAFT** — First pass, not fact-checked
2. **REVIEWED** — Self-edited, sources verified
3. **READY_TO_PUBLISH** — Polished, formatted
4. **PUBLISHED** — Live on blog

**Update publishing_tracker.csv:**
```csv
Title,Status,Date_Written,Date_Published,URL,Platform
"5 Ways Depression Changed My Teaching",DRAFT,2026-06-02,,"",blog
"How to Automate Job Applications",READY_TO_PUBLISH,2026-05-15,2026-06-07,"https://...",blog
"Navigating Work with Bipolar II",PUBLISHED,2026-05-01,2026-05-28,"https://...",blog|linkedin
```

### Publishing Strategy

**Weekly rhythm:**
- Monday: Run automation, update sheets
- Tuesday-Wednesday: Create (if energy allows) OR rest
- Friday: Publish 1 banked essay
- Weekend: Plan next week

This decouples creation from publishing. No pressure.

## Preventing Burnout & Cycling

### Watch for Hypomania Burnout Trap

You feel great → You overcommit → You crash

**Red flags:**
- Writing for 12+ hours daily
- Saying yes to every collaboration
- Minimal sleep (4-5 hours, feeling fine)
- Grandiose plans (publish daily, reach 100k people)

**Prevention:**
- Max 4 essays per high-energy week (not 10)
- Max 5 new commitments per month
- Sleep 7+ hours even if not tired
- Review plans with therapist/partner

### Watch for Depression Trap

You're depressed → You skip meds/therapy → You get worse

**Red flags:**
- Isolation (no messages for 1+ week)
- Skipping therapy appointments
- Changed sleep/eating
- Thoughts of self-harm

**Prevention:**
- Therapy is non-negotiable (stay scheduled)
- Meds are non-negotiable
- Health check-in weekly (same day, same time)
- Tell therapist about creative goals (not just health)

## Talking to Collaborators & Employers

### What to Disclose (Your Choice)

You DO NOT owe anyone your diagnosis. But you might benefit from:
- Trusted collaborators knowing: "I have cycles, I plan around them"
- Therapist knowing: "I'm writing to build exposure and find work"
- Employer knowing: Depends on legal situation + relationship

### What to Say

**To collaborators:**
> "I have a condition that affects my energy levels. I work in cycles—high-output periods followed by rest. I plan projects around this to maintain quality. Can we schedule deadlines with 2-week buffers?"

**To therapist:**
> "I want to write essays and build my reputation. I'm worried about burnout with bipolar II. Can we talk about a sustainable pace?"

**To employer:**
> "I manage a chronic condition with regular treatment. I'm reliable and plan my commitments carefully. Flexible scheduling helps me perform best."

## When Things Fall Apart

### If You Skip Meds
- Call prescriber same day
- Update health log
- Tell therapist
- Don't punish yourself

### If Depression Hits Hard
- It's not permanent
- It's not failure
- Publishing stops, meds continue
- Call therapist, tell them

### If You Crash After Hypomania
- This is expected
- Reduce workload 50%
- Increase sleep (10+ hrs if needed)
- Tell collaborators you're taking a week

### If You Miss a Week of Writing
- It's fine
- You have banked content
- Publish something anyway (old essay OK)
- Return to schedule next week
