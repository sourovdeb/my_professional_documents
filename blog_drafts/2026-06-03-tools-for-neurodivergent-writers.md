# The Neurodivergent Writer's Toolkit: What Actually Works (2026)

I am going to save you some time. Most "productivity tool" roundups are written by people with executive function who are trying to optimize. This one is written by someone with ADHD and bipolar disorder who is trying to survive.

Different problem. Different tools.

Here is what I actually use, why I use it, and what it costs (most of it: nothing).

---

## For Writing

**Obsidian** (free) — All my drafts live here. Local files, no subscription, works offline. For a brain that forgets what it was doing the moment the internet goes down, local storage is not a preference, it is a requirement. The `[[wikilink]]` system means I can connect ideas without having to remember where I put them.

**LanguageTool** (free tier) — Better than Grammarly for multilingual writers. I switch between English and French in the same document and it does not panic. The free tier catches the errors that brain fog creates.

**Hemingway Editor** (free, browser) — Paste your draft in and it highlights every passive sentence, every adverb, every thing you do to hide that you are not sure what you are saying. Brutal. Useful.

---

## For Focus

**Forest** (free tier) — You plant a tree. The tree dies if you pick up your phone. This sounds childish and it works completely. For a dopamine-seeking brain, the visual cost of breaking focus is more effective than any reminder.

**Goblin Tools** (free) — Break any task into micro-steps. I use the "Magic To Do" feature when I am staring at a task that my brain reads as a single wall of impossibility. Type "write blog post," ask it to break this down into seven steps, suddenly I can start. 

---

## For Scheduling and Stability

**Tiimo** (free tier) — Visual daily planner designed for neurodivergent users. Not a to-do list. A schedule with colour and duration, so I can see my day rather than read it. Seeing it is the difference between doing it and forgetting it exists.

**Leantime** (open source) — For those who want a project management tool that actually accounts for how neurodivergent brains work. Self-hostable, which means no subscription and no data selling.

---

## For Automation (For Writers Who Hate Admin)

**n8n** (open source, self-hostable) — This is the honest answer to "I want to automate my publishing workflow without paying $50/month." n8n connects your apps with visual workflows. I use it to move draft files from one place to another automatically. The learning curve is real but the payoff is permanent.

**Otter.ai** (free tier) — Speak your first draft. I am a much better talker than typist when I am dysregulated. Otter transcribes and timestamps. I edit the transcript. Five hundred words in twenty minutes instead of two hours.

---

## The Honest Rule

No tool will substitute for the fundamental work of understanding your own cycles and protecting your sleep. But the right tools reduce the friction between having an idea and getting it out of your head.

That friction is the enemy. Every tool here attacks it.
