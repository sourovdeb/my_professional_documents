# Example: How I Manage Email When Bipolar

**Date:** 2026-06-03  
**Status:** Ready  
**Word Count:** 487  
**Energy Level Used:** 7

---

## Hook

Depression makes email pile up. Mania makes you reply to everything at 3 AM. I've lost jobs over both. Here's what actually works.

---

## Context

Email is survival. It's how employers, collaborators, and friends reach you. But bipolar disorder warps your relationship with email—sometimes you ignore it for weeks; sometimes you hyperfocus and send regrettable messages. According to SAMHSA, mood disorders affect decision-making across all life domains, including communication.

**Sources used:**
- [SAMHSA Mental Health Overview](https://www.samhsa.gov/)
- [Mayo Clinic: Bipolar Disorder Symptoms](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/bipolar-disorder/symptoms-causes/syc-20355955)

---

## Main Insight

I use three rules:

**Rule 1: The 24-hour delay.** Don't send emails when you feel rushed or emotional. Write it, save as draft, wait 24 hours. If you still want to send it tomorrow, send it. This stops manic emails and shame-deleting later.

**Rule 2: Folder triage.** One folder: "Requires Action." Everything else can wait. During depressive episodes, I only look at that folder. Action items get a 48-hour window—no guilt if I miss it.

**Rule 3: Voice notes first.** When you can't write (depression), voice-note your reply, then write it. This forces you to think through what you actually mean.

These aren't productivity hacks—they're mental health protection. You can't optimize yourself into wellness.

---

## Takeaway

If you manage bipolar disorder, email isn't optional. Neither is protecting your mood while using it. Pick one rule this week—the 24-hour delay works for most people. Notice what changes.
