# Exposure Without Social Media — 2026 Research

Target: readers/clients reachable *without* Instagram/TikTok/X. Skips algorithmic-feed tactics; favors email, RSS, print, and in-person channels. Complements [02_EXPOSURE](02_EXPOSURE.md).

## Mental health / neurodivergent storytelling
- **Platforms:** paid Substack (NeuroStack, Neurodivergent Insights, ADHD Unpacked), lit-mags (Blanket Sea, Barking Sycamores, The Perch, Please See Me), ADDitude Magazine, NAMI Blog "Share Your Story."
- **Audience:** newsletter readers skew flat across ages (28–32%, Pew 2026) — genuinely reaches non-social, 30–55, disability/therapy-community readers. No English-language mental-health outlet found for Réunion/France; better fit there is expat newsletters (FUSAC-style). Bengali diaspora: radio + community newsletters outperform digital lit-mags.
- **Channels:** guest essays on NAMI/ADDitude/therapy-practice blogs; podcast guesting (Sparking Wholeness, LatinX Therapy); mail-based zines (Draw Poorly, Collide) via Etsy/zine distros.
- **Trend:** anti-AI-slop backlash is rewarding verifiably human, lived-experience writing; paid newsletters framed as "slow media."

## English teaching (CELTA / Business English)
- **Platforms:** TEFL.com, TEFLwork, Dave's ESL Cafe, Bridge — discoverable trainer profiles schools search directly. LinkedIn as professional directory, not social feed.
- **Audience:** corporate L&D (37%+ multinationals now run custom language programs), CPF/OPCO-funded French adult learners, exam-prep (TOEIC/IELTS) segment, Réunion's sector English (tourism, logistics).
- **Channels:** cold email/LOI to school directors & OPCOs; flyers at CCI offices, mairies, coworking spaces; Franco-British Chamber of Commerce partnership; IATEFL / TESOL France Colloquium (Paris, Nov 2026) for in-person networking; referrals (~90% of income for successful independents, per research).
- **2026 gate:** Qualiopi certification is now required to access CPF/OPCO funding — without it, most corporate/individual buyers can't pay. Pair with a recognized cert (TOEIC/Linguaskill).

## General blogging (immigration, teaching, AI, philosophy)
- **Platforms:** Substack (8.4M paid subs, +68% YoY) for essay/newsletter hybrid; personal domain + RSS + email (Buttondown); HackerNoon (4M readers, 12 languages) for tech/AI pieces; Hacker News for tech essays.
- **Audience:** RSS adoption +34% YoY among privacy-conscious 30s–50s fleeing algorithmic feeds; immigrant/diaspora communities have a strong blogs-as-identity tradition — a natural fit for the immigration story.
- **Channels:** library programs and immigrant-writer associations (live readings); postcard/mail subscription clubs (real 25→1,600 subscriber case cited); niche non-feed forums (Discourse/subreddits) for seeding word-of-mouth.
- **Trend:** AI Overviews cut small-publisher search traffic 47–60%, pushing writers toward owned channels; Google now surfaces "subscribed publications" in AI Overviews, rewarding a real newsletter relationship.

## Cross-cutting unconventional tactics
- Physical mail: postcard/letter clubs are having a real 2026 revival — cheap, loyal, non-algorithmic.
- One newsletter, three audiences: segment sends (mental-health story / teaching updates / general essays) from a single owned list instead of three separate platforms.
- Community partnerships beat cold platforms: therapy practices, Chambers of Commerce, and immigrant-writer associations all offer built-in trusted audiences.
- Print/zine and radio (Bengali-language) remain the strongest non-digital channel for reaching readers who are offline by choice, not by circumstance.
