# 09 — Exposure Research (2026 update)

Fresh research to sharpen [02_EXPOSURE](02_EXPOSURE.md). Short by design — see Gaps below for what needs deeper digging.

## Platform fit by content area

| Area | Best platforms | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Mental health writing | Substack (niche/paid subs up 138% YoY), Medium (*Human Parts*, *The Ascent*), Reddit (r/bipolar, r/ADHD, r/CPTSD), Vox First Person / Glamour for paid pitches | Readers want first-hand, specific stories; publications still pay ~$500/piece |
| English teaching | TikTok (single-topic, ≤10min clear lessons), LinkedIn (younger audience now, video growing), niche Substack/newsletter for serious learners | ELT market ~$95B in 2026; short clear-goal videos outperform |
| General blogging | Owned site/WordPress as hub + Substack spoke; LinkedIn for reach | Substack open rates 40–60% vs 21.5% industry average |

## Audience & region notes

- **Mental health:** Gen Z/Millennial-skewed on Substack; Reddit gives tight, rules-bound communities — value first, link rarely.
- **English teaching:** Asia-Pacific = ~42% of global ELT demand (China, Korea, Japan, Vietnam, Thailand); Latin America fast-growing — ~80% of pro jobs there need English, only ~20% of workers qualify (Brazil, Mexico, Chile, Argentina). Africa/Central Asia are the next growth wave.
- **Blogging:** No single dominant region; niche specificity (owning one corner of a topic) beats broad appeal everywhere.

## Channels worth using now

- Substack Notes + Recommendations (primary growth engine under 10k subs — beats cold outreach)
- LinkedIn native posts (link in comments, not the post body)
- TikTok/Reels for ESL micro-lessons; cross-post to Instagram
- Reddit niche subs (support-first, not promo)
- Guest pitches to established pubs once 5–10 strong pieces exist

## Unconventional tactics

- **Physical mail:** postcards for re-engagement/renewal of existing subscribers, not cold acquisition — works best behavior-triggered (e.g., "haven't opened in 60 days")
- **Newsletter cross-promos:** swap recommendations with 2–3 peer writers in the same niche instead of paid ads
- **Community partnerships:** affiliate-list swaps with adjacent orgs (e.g., a wellbeing newsletter partnering with a local ESL school) — mutual audience share, no cost

## Gaps / follow-up needed

- English-teaching numbers are TEFL-*job-market* stats, not content-audience stats — a separate search on "ESL learner social media habits" would sharpen this
