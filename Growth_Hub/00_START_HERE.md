## Five rules that run through all seven

1. **Quality over quantity.** One sharp 500-word piece beats five rushed ones. Better never has to mean more.
2. **Active voice, human tone.** "I tested this" — not "this was tested." Write like you talk to a smart friend. Never monotone.
3. **One piece, many places.** Write once; reshape for LinkedIn, Medium, Substack. You already did this with the CELTA essay — that is the template, not the exception.
4. **The system carries the day the person can't.** Every routine here is built for the crash day. If a step needs motivation, it is designed wrong.
5. **Only official and tested sources.** Claims trace to a real source. Ideas come from things you actually lived or verified — that is your edge over every AI-written feed.

## Your unfair advantages

- A **real, hard-won story**: immigration, teaching, neurodiversity, surviving a system. AI can fake polish; it cannot fake having lived it.
- A **proven viewpoint that travels**: the CELTA piece works because it is honest, specific, and useful. Repeat that formula.
- A **niche you own**: English teaching + AI + neurodivergent learning + the French system. Almost nobody sits at that exact crossroads.
