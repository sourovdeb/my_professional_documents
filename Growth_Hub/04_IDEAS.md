# 04 — Ideas

**Goal: never face a blank page. Capture ideas when they come; pull from the bank when you're empty.**

ADHD does not lack ideas — it loses them. So the rule from your Daily Living Strategy applies: **don't store ideas in your head. Store them in the system.**

## Capture (the always-open inbox)

- Keep one note — `Growth_Hub/IDEA_INBOX.md` or an Obsidian inbox note — open on phone and desktop.
- An idea hits → one line, unsorted, immediately. No tidying at capture time.
- Once a week, on a stable day, move the good ones into the Topic Bank below. Delete the rest without guilt.

## Your four territories (the niche you own)

Every strong piece sits in one of these. They are yours because you have lived them:

1. **Teaching English** — methods, exams, what actually works in a classroom.
2. **Neurodivergence & learning** — ADHD/bipolar, and how brains really learn and work.
3. **AI & education / work** — where the CELTA piece landed; honest, first-hand.
4. **The immigrant / French-system experience** — navigating MDPH, jobs, life in France.

The sweet spot is where two overlap (e.g. *AI × teaching* = your CELTA hit; *ADHD × teaching* = nearly untouched).

## Where ideas may come from (only tested, legal sources)

- **Your own life and logs** — the richest, most original vein. The Daily Living Strategy, your transcripts, your job hunt are all raw material.
- **Your students' real questions** — each one is a "how-to" piece.
- **The comments on your published work** — readers tell you what they want next.
- **Official, citable sources** for anything factual — NIMH, NHS, HAS, Ameli, Cambridge English, gov.uk / service-public.fr. Link them; never invent a statistic.
- **What you genuinely read and verified** — not trends you didn't check, not AI-hallucinated "facts".

## The "second piece" rule

Every published piece contains the seed of the next. When you finish one, write the next idea straight into the inbox before you stand up. Momentum beats inspiration.
