# 03 — Tools

**Goal: a small, mostly-free toolkit that writes, designs, and removes friction — chosen for an ADHD brain and low-energy days.**

Rule: **fewer tools, fully learned.** Every new app is a tax on attention. Pick one per job, learn it once on a stable day, then stop shopping.

## Write & think

| Tool | Job | Why it fits you | Cost |
|------|-----|-----------------|------|
| **Obsidian** | Your "one board / external brain" — notes, drafts, idea capture, all in plain `.md` | Local, private, plain-text (matches this repo). Runs on Linux, phone, everything. This is the *one place* the Daily Living Strategy asks for. | Free for personal use |
| **LanguageTool** | Grammar + style checker (catches passive voice, wordiness) | Browser add-on; works in EN and FR — useful for your bilingual writing. | Free tier is enough |
| **Hemingway Editor** | Flags passive voice, long sentences, hard-to-read lines | Forces the active, human voice from [01_WRITE](01_WRITE.md). Paste, fix the red, done. | Free (web) |
| **A local LLM (Ollama)** | Drafting partner, outline help, "tighten this paragraph" | You already built around this in the repo. Private, no API cost, runs offline. Use it to *edit*, never to write your story for you. | Free |

> Write in Obsidian → paste into Hemingway to tighten → publish. That is the whole writing stack.

## Design (covers, banners, simple graphics)

| Tool | Job | Why | Cost |
|------|-----|-----|------|
| **Canva** | Article covers, LinkedIn banners, quote cards, simple slides | Template-based — you start from something that already looks good, low executive-function cost. | Free tier covers nearly everything |
| **Excalidraw** | Quick hand-drawn-style diagrams (e.g. your CELTA "3 pillars") | Free, no account, instant. Great for explaining an idea visually. | Free |
| **Unsplash / Pexels** | Free, legally reusable photos for headers | No licensing worry — both are free-to-use. Always pick legal sources. | Free |

A consistent look = recognition. Make **one** Canva template for your article covers (your name, same font, same colour) and reuse it every time. Don't redesign per post.

## Carry the load (automation & admin)

You already own strong scaffolding in this repo — reuse it, don't rebuild it:

- **Gmail Bulk Draft Automation** (`background.js`, `sidepanel.js`, the draft scripts) — for batched, personalised job/outreach emails.
- **SMART_EMAIL_COMPOSER_v1.gs** (Google Apps Script) — your job-automation composer.
- **Reusable skills** in `Story_of_Sourov/04_REUSABLE_SKILLS/` — recycle these instead of writing new ones.

For scheduling consistency without willpower:
- **Phone alarms / OS reminders** — the "two alarms fire the routine for you" rule.
- **`systemd --user` timers on Linux** — already named in your Daily Living Strategy for meds + wind-down. Add one for the daily writing block.
- **A platform's native scheduler** (LinkedIn, Substack) — write in a hyperfocus window, schedule it to publish on a flat day. The machine remembers so you don't have to.

## What NOT to add

- No second note app. No third social platform. No paid "all-in-one creator suite" you'll abandon in a week.
- No tool that needs daily logins or daily upkeep to stay useful. If it breaks on a crash day, it's the wrong tool.

**Total recurring cost of this stack: €0.** Upgrade only a tool you already use daily and outgrew.
