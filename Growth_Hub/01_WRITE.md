# 01 — Write

**Goal: one finished 500-word piece, most days. Quality over streak.**

500 words is deliberate. It is long enough to say something real, short enough to finish in one focused block (one 25-minute timer, sometimes two). On a crash day, you write nothing and that is fine — see [Consistency](06_CONSISTENCY.md).

## The daily 500 — a repeatable shape

Most strong short pieces follow the same skeleton. Fill it in; do not reinvent it.

1. **Hook (1–2 lines).** A claim, a confession, or a sharp question. Your CELTA piece opened with *"I did the CELTA… and I think it has a serious problem nobody's talking about."* That is the move.
2. **Context (2–3 lines).** What is this, for the reader who has never heard of it. One breath, no jargon.
3. **The turn (the body).** Your actual point. Use a short list or arrows (→) so a tired reader can skim and still get it.
4. **The fix or the lesson.** Do not just complain or describe — leave the reader with something usable.
5. **The open door (last line).** A question to the reader. It invites comments, which the platforms reward.

## How to write it well — the voice rules

- **Active voice.** "Employers can't tell the difference" beats "a difference cannot be told." Subject does the verb. Hunt down every *"was / were / is being"* and ask if a person can do that verb instead.
- **Human, not monotone.** Vary sentence length. A long, winding sentence that builds — then a short one. That rhythm is what makes it sound alive.
- **Concrete over abstract.** "Run the video through an AI transcription tool, get perfect answers in 5 minutes" beats "assessments are vulnerable to misuse."
- **One idea per piece.** If you have two, you have two pieces. Save the second.
- **Cut the warm-up.** Your first paragraph is usually throat-clearing. Delete it and start at the second.
- **Read it out loud.** If you stumble, the sentence is wrong. Your ear is the best editor you have.

## The 500-word session (one timer)

```
0–5 min   : Pick ONE idea from the Topic Bank (04_IDEAS). Do not browse.
5–25 min  : Write the whole draft. Ugly is fine. Do NOT edit while writing.
break     : Stand up. Water. Look out a window.
25–40 min : Cut 20%. Fix passive voice. Read aloud once. Add the closing question.
done      : Save to /Growth_Hub/drafts/ . Publish today or tomorrow — not "someday".
```

If you only have energy for the first 20 minutes, save the ugly draft. A draft you can finish later beats a perfect piece you never started.

## One piece, many places (your repurpose engine)

You already proved this: the CELTA essay became a LinkedIn post, a Medium article, and a Substack post (see `/Communications/`). Lock that as your standard workflow:

| Place | Reshape it into | Length |
|-------|-----------------|--------|
| **LinkedIn** | Punchy hook, short lines, lots of white space, end on a question | 150–300 words |
| **Medium / Substack** | The full essay with a TL;DR at the top | 500–1000 words |
| **X / Threads** | The single sharpest sentence as a standalone post + link | 1–2 lines |

Write the long version once. Cut it down for the others. Never write three things; write one and shrink it.

## A light style checklist (run before publishing)

- [ ] First line earns the second.
- [ ] No paragraph longer than 4 lines.
- [ ] Zero unnecessary passive voice.
- [ ] One clear takeaway a reader could repeat to a friend.
- [ ] Every fact traces to something you lived or a source you can link.
- [ ] Ends with a question or a door left open.
