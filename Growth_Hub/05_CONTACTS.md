# 05 — Contacts

**Goal: turn readers and outreach into jobs and useful relationships — without spam, and without spending energy you don't have.**

You already built the machinery for this. Reuse it; don't rebuild it:
- `Story_of_Sourov/.../MASTER_CONTACT_DIRECTORY.md` — your contact list.
- `CONTACTS_AND_EMAILS_FOUR_CHANNELS.md` — your four-channel approach.
- The Gmail bulk-draft system (`background.js`, draft scripts) — batched, personalised outreach.

## The two kinds of contact

1. **Inbound** — people who find you through your writing ([02_EXPOSURE](02_EXPOSURE.md)). Warmest leads you can get. Make them easy to act on.
2. **Outbound** — people you reach first (schools, recruiters, fellow teachers, editors). Higher effort, so batch it and personalise it.

## Make inbound effortless

- Every profile (LinkedIn, Substack about, repo README) states **who you are + what you offer + how to reach you**, in one line each.
- When someone comments or messages, reply within a day if you can. A real reply turns a reader into a contact.
- Keep a simple "contact me" path: one email, one LinkedIn. Don't scatter five channels.

## Outbound, the humane way (quality over quantity)

The opposite of mass spam. **10 genuinely personal messages beat 200 generic ones** — and your batch tools make 10 personal ones cheap.

A reusable 4-line template (fill the brackets):
```
Hi [Name],
[One specific, true line showing you know who they are — their school, a post they wrote.]
[One line on what you do and why it's relevant to them — CELTA-certified English trainer, IELTS/TOEIC/Business.]
[A small, low-pressure ask — a quick call, a question, "is your team hiring?"]
Either way, thanks for your time. — Sourov
```
Personalise line 2 every time. The batch system handles the rest, but never let it remove the human touch — that's the whole point.

## The follow-up system (where most people quit)

- Log every meaningful contact: name, channel, date, what was said, **next action + date**. Reuse the Master Contact Directory.
- One follow-up after ~7 days if no reply: short, friendly, no guilt-tripping. Then let it rest.
- Visible, not searchable: keep "who am I waiting on?" on one screen, like the Daily Living Strategy's anchor card.

## Routed by state (so it survives a bad day)

- **Stable / level:** write the personal outreach lines, send the batch, do live replies.
- **Elevated / hyperfocus:** draft *many* outreach messages and schedule them — stock the larder.
- **Flat / low:** reply only to warm inbound; one message max.
- **Crash:** nothing. Contacts wait. The follow-up log remembers for you.

## Boundaries

- Disclose your condition only when *you* choose to and it serves you (a job's needs, RQTH support). [07_WELLBEING](07_WELLBEING.md) and your `SKILL_neurodiversity-disclosure-documentation.md` cover the how.
- Never buy lists or cold-blast strangers. The right contacts come from real work seen by real people.
